## Paragraph-by-paragraph deep notes

### ¶1 PARTIES

- **Legal function.** Identifies the contracting parties (Seller(s) and Buyer(s)) whose legal capacity binds the contract. "Seller agrees to sell and convey … and Buyer agrees to buy" — the mutual-promise core. Without identifiable parties of legal capacity, no contract exists (offer + acceptance + consideration + competent parties).
- **Authority source.** Common law of contracts; no specific TREC rule or statute mandates this paragraph's content beyond TREC's promulgation under 22 TAC §537.28 and TRELA §1101.155. The "joint and several" implication for multiple parties is governed by case law on multi-party contracts.
- **Who fills it, when.** The license holder who drafts the offer (typically the buyer's agent for a buyer's offer; the listing agent if the seller is the offeror in a counter). Filled at contract-drafting time, BEFORE any signature.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Using nicknames or shortened names ("Johnny Smith" instead of "John A. Smith, Jr.") — clouds title and forces a corrective amendment (39-9) at closing. Source: LegalClarity, "TREC 1-4 Family Residential Contract Explained" (https://legalclarity.org/trec-1-4-family-residential-contract-explained/, retrieved 2026-06-20).
  2. Omitting the marital recital where one spouse holds title. Texas community-property rules (Family Code §3.002) mean a married seller frequently needs the non-titled spouse to join the conveyance regardless of who holds record title to homestead. Failure to include the joining spouse can void the closing.
  3. Naming an entity (LLC, trust) without identifying the authorized signer / trustee in the signature block.
- **Cross-reference.** ¶24 signature block must match ¶1 party identity exactly. ¶21 Notices block addresses are tied to these named parties. Page 10 broker block identifies which BROKER represents which of these parties.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `seller_names`, `buyer_names` (one logical field each — the contract permits "John Smith and Jane Smith" as one Seller party string). Per Hadley's locked widget conventions: do not split into `seller_1_name` / `seller_2_name` unless the rendered PDF has two separate widgets for them. Verify against PDF before splitting.

### ¶2 PROPERTY

- **Legal function.** Defines the "Property" — the conveyance subject matter. Texas law requires a writing that adequately identifies the realty to satisfy the Statute of Frauds (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §26.01(b)(4)). Inadequate description = unenforceable contract.
- **Authority source.** Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §26.01 (Statute of Frauds); Tex. Prop. Code Ch. 5 (Conveyances generally); 22 TAC §537.28 (form promulgation). The "permanently installed and built-in items" language reflects common-law Texas fixture doctrine.
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **2.A LAND** — legal description: Lot, Block, Addition, City, County (Texas), known as (street address/zip), or as described on attached exhibit (for metes-and-bounds or condo unit descriptions inappropriate here).
  - **2.B IMPROVEMENTS** — fixtures and improvements deemed included by default: house, garage, all permanently installed and built-in items (lists 20+ examples including HVAC, water softener, chandeliers, garage door openers, etc.). **2025 addition: "generators"** added per BLC recommendation (TREC, "Form Changes Effective January 3, 2025," https://www.trec.texas.gov/article/trec-form-changes-effective-january-3-2025).
  - **2.C ACCESSORIES** — non-fixtures that convey unless excluded: window units, stoves, fireplace screens, curtains/rods, blinds, window shades, draperies/rods, door keys, mailbox keys, pool keys, pool equipment + maintenance accessories, artificial fireplace logs, security systems that are NOT fixtures, and "Controls" (i) garage doors, (ii) entry gates, (iii) other improvements/accessories — including Seller's transferable rights to software/applications used to access and control improvements/accessories AND the hardware used solely to control them.
  - **2.D EXCLUSIONS** — items the Seller is retaining (must be removed prior to delivery of possession).
  - **2.E RESERVATIONS** — "Any reservation for oil, gas, or other minerals, water, timber, or other interests is made in accordance with an attached addendum." This is the trigger for TREC 44-3 Addendum for Reservation of Oil, Gas, and Other Minerals.
- **Who fills it, when.** Licensee drafting the contract pulls legal description from the listing's source (county appraisal district + title commitment ideally; MLS as a starting point). Time: contract drafting.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Pulling legal description from the MLS without cross-verifying CAD or title commitment — MLS frequently shortens or misstates the legal. Texas Title, "Paragraph 2 of One to Four Family Residential Contract Explained" (https://texastitle.com/paragraph-2-of-one-to-four-family-residential-contract-explained/, retrieved 2026-06-20).
  2. Buyers assuming wall-mounted TV / mounts / cameras / appliances stay because they are "attached." If not listed in 2.C (accessories) and the seller wants to keep them, they MUST go in 2.D (exclusions) or a fixture fight erupts pre-closing. Elena Garrett, "TREC One to Four Family Residential Contract — Plain-English Guide for Buyers (2026)" (https://www.elenagarrett.com/, retrieved 2026-06-20).
  3. Failing to attach the TREC 44-3 mineral-reservation addendum when 2.E reservation language is activated. Without the addendum, the reservation has no operative terms.
  4. Listing the refrigerator in ¶11 special provisions rather than as a fixture/accessory addition or under the (proper) field. The refrigerator is NOT in the 2.B fixtures list and NOT in the 2.C accessories list — if the parties want it to convey, the cleanest place is a typed addition to 2.C or a TREC 39-9 amendment after execution if late-discovered.
- **Cross-reference.** Triggers TREC 44-3 (mineral reservation) via 2.E. The "smart device" requirement in ¶10.B Possession ties to the "Controls" language in 2.C — Seller must hand over the credentials to the smart devices and disconnect Seller's personal devices.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `property_lot`, `property_block`, `property_addition`, `property_city`, `property_county`, `property_address_zip` for 2.A; `property_exclusions` for 2.D (free-text); 2.B and 2.C are fixed contract text with no fill blanks; 2.E reservation language activation is signaled by attaching TREC 44-3 (the ¶22 addendum checkbox `addendum_oil_gas_minerals`).

### ¶3 SALES PRICE

- **Legal function.** Quantifies consideration. Together with parties (¶1) and property (¶2), satisfies the essential terms required for an enforceable contract under Texas Statute of Frauds (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §26.01).
- **Authority source.** Common-law contract doctrine; TRELA §1101.155 form promulgation authority. The "Sum of all financing described in the attached" language is operationally tied to the financing-type addenda promulgated under 22 TAC §537.30 (Third Party Financing), §537.31 (Loan Assumption), §537.32 (Seller Financing) etc.
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **3.A** Cash portion of Sales Price payable by Buyer at closing $ ____. "Cash portion of the Sales Price" does NOT include proceeds from borrowing of any kind or selling other real property except as disclosed in this contract.
  - **3.B** Sum of all financing described in the attached: ☐ Third Party Financing Addendum, ☐ Loan Assumption Addendum, ☐ Seller Financing Addendum … $ ____.
  - **3.C** Sales Price (Sum of A and B) $ ____.
- **Who fills it, when.** Buyer's agent drafting the offer. Time: contract drafting. Math must reconcile exactly — A + B must equal C, or escrow/lender will reject the contract at file open.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Failing to check ANY box in 3.B when financing is involved — leaves the contract ambiguous about which financing addendum governs.
  2. Checking multiple 3.B boxes when only one financing arrangement is contemplated (the form allows it on its face but practical use is single-select).
  3. A+B ≠ C math errors. Lender's loan amount in the TPF addendum (40-11) also must reconcile back to 3.B.
  4. Listing the full sales price in 3.A and zero in 3.B when financing is actually being used — defeats the financing contingency in 40-11.
- **Cross-reference.** Each 3.B checkbox triggers a separate mandatory addendum: TPF box → TREC 40-11; Loan Assumption box → TREC 39-10; Seller Financing box → TREC 26-9. The TPF box also typically pulls in TREC 49-1 (Addendum Concerning Right to Terminate Due to Lender's Appraisal), which is OPTIONAL but commonly attached. Sales price changes after appraisal that drop the loan amount typically trigger a TREC 39-9 (Amendment) plus the 49-1 right.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `sales_price_cash_portion` (3.A), `sales_price_financed` (3.B sum), `sales_price_total` (3.C). Each 3.B checkbox is a SEPARATE key (mutually-exclusive N-way convention, NOT paired Y/N): `addendum_third_party_financing`, `addendum_loan_assumption`, `addendum_seller_financing`. Application logic enforces single-select.

### ¶4 LEASES

- **Legal function.** Surfaces existing leases that survive closing and bind the Buyer as successor landlord, and natural-resource leases (mineral, geothermal, wind) that affect Buyer's surface and subsurface rights.
- **Authority source.** Texas Property Code §92 chapter (residential tenancies) governs the residential-lease pass-through. The "geothermal" addition is tied to the 2023 Texas legislative recognition that property owners own geothermal energy below their land's surface (per TREC's January 2025 change recap: https://www.trec.texas.gov/article/trec-form-changes-effective-january-3-2025).
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **4.A RESIDENTIAL LEASES** ☐ — Property subject to one or more residential leases; Addendum Regarding Residential Leases is attached.
  - **4.B FIXTURE LEASES** ☐ — Fixtures on the Property subject to one or more fixture leases (solar panels, propane tanks, water softener, security system); Addendum Regarding Fixture Leases is attached.
  - **4.C NATURAL RESOURCE LEASES** ☐ — "Natural Resource Lease" means existing oil and gas, mineral, geothermal, water, wind, or other natural resource lease affecting the Property to which Seller is a party. **Paired (1)/(2) HAS / has-NOT delivered checkboxes**, plus "____ days" blank in (2) for Buyer's termination right if Seller fails to deliver within 3 days after Effective Date.
- **Who fills it, when.** Listing agent / Seller at offer-stage, but ALL four 4.A/4.B/4.C/4.C(2) checkboxes get checked at contract drafting by the agent preparing the contract based on Seller's disclosures.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Forgetting the FIXTURE LEASE box for solar panels. Solar lease assumption requirements + UCC-1 filings on the panels routinely surface late, blowing closings. This is one of the highest-frequency post-execution disputes for 2024-2025 transactions.
  2. Checking 4.A but not attaching the Addendum Regarding Residential Leases — leaves Buyer with no operative terms about how the lease passes through.
  3. Mis-mapping the paired (1)/(2) checkboxes in 4.C as separate booleans rather than as a Y/N pair. **Locked Hadley convention:** both checkboxes share key `natural_resource_leases_delivered`; engine inverts (true → check (1) HAS delivered; false → check (2) has-NOT delivered + write the days blank).
- **Cross-reference.** 4.A triggers TREC Addendum Regarding Residential Leases; 4.B triggers TREC Addendum Regarding Fixture Leases (note: these addenda exist and ¶22 lists them as separate checkboxes). 4.C reservation/lease language ties to ¶2.E reservation and may trigger TREC 44-3 if Seller is retaining minerals.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `residential_leases_present` (4.A), `fixture_leases_present` (4.B), `natural_resource_leases_present` (4.C), `natural_resource_leases_delivered` (paired Y/N in 4.C(1)/(2)), `natural_resource_leases_termination_days` (4.C(2) days blank).

### ¶5 EARNEST MONEY AND TERMINATION OPTION

- **Legal function.** Establishes (i) the earnest-money deposit (a good-faith deposit held in escrow, credited to sales price at closing, forfeitable on Buyer default), and (ii) the option fee for the unrestricted right to terminate during the option period (an independent unilateral option separately purchased for nominal consideration). These are TWO LEGALLY DISTINCT INSTRUMENTS handled in one paragraph.
- **Authority source.** Common-law option doctrine; case law confirming the option fee is consideration for Seller's promise to leave the offer open (e.g., Culbertson v. Brodsky, 788 S.W.2d 156 (Tex. App. — Fort Worth 1990)). 22 TAC §537.28 (form promulgation). The 2021 revision moved option-fee delivery from "to Seller" to "to Escrow Agent" — see TREC, "Changes to Delivery of Option Fee" (https://www.trec.texas.gov/article/changes-delivery-option-fee-0, retrieved 2026-06-20) and Texas REALTORS magazine, "Changes to TREC Contracts and Option Fee Delivery," March 2021 (https://www.texasrealestate.com/members/communications/texas-realtor-magazine/issues/march-2021/changes-to-trec-contracts-and-option-fee-delivery/).
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **5.A DELIVERY OF EARNEST MONEY AND OPTION FEE.** Within 3 days after Effective Date, Buyer must deliver to ____ (Escrow Agent) at ____ (address): $____ as earnest money and $____ as Option Fee. May be combined in a single payment.
    - **5.A(1)** Additional earnest money $____ within ____ days after Effective Date.
    - **5.A(2)** **2025-new "Legal Holiday" extension language** — "If the last day to deliver the earnest money, Option Fee, or the additional earnest money falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday, the time to deliver … is extended until the end of the next day that is not a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday." (TREC noted this as a 2025 clarification.)
    - **5.A(3)** Application order: amounts received are applied first to Option Fee, then to earnest money, then to additional earnest money.
    - **5.A(4)** Buyer authorizes Escrow Agent to release Option Fee to Seller at any time without further notice or consent; releases Escrow Agent from liability. Option Fee credited to Sales Price at closing.
  - **5.B TERMINATION OPTION.** For nominal consideration (Option Fee), and Buyer's agreement to pay the Option Fee within time required, Seller grants Buyer **unrestricted right to terminate** by giving notice of termination to Seller within ____ days after Effective Date (Option Period). **Notices under this paragraph must be given by 5:00 p.m. (local time where Property is located) by the date specified.** If Buyer gives notice within time, Option Fee will NOT be refunded; Escrow Agent shall release any Option Fee remaining with Escrow Agent to Seller; and any earnest money will be refunded to Buyer.
  - **5.C FAILURE TO TIMELY DELIVER EARNEST MONEY.** Seller may terminate or exercise default remedies under ¶15 (or both) by notice to Buyer before Buyer delivers.
  - **5.D FAILURE TO TIMELY DELIVER OPTION FEE.** If no dollar amount stated OR Buyer fails to timely deliver, Buyer does NOT have unrestricted right to terminate under ¶5.
  - **5.E TIME.** Time is of the essence; strict compliance with the time for performance required.
- **Who fills it, when.** Buyer's agent drafts the dollar amounts and days. Escrow Agent name/address is the title company or attorney handling escrow. Time: contract drafting.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Confusing earnest money and option fee. Earnest money is REFUNDABLE if Buyer timely terminates under ¶5 option; Option Fee is NEVER refunded. Source: Texas REALTORS, "Changes to TREC Contracts and Option Fee Delivery."
  2. Missing the 5:00 p.m. local-time deadline. TREC FAQ: "We are selling our house and the buyer never paid the option fee … what happens now?" — Buyer who never paid the option fee does NOT have the unrestricted right to terminate (5.D). The option period clock STILL RUNS per 5.B, but without 5.D termination right.
  3. Leaving the Option Fee dollar blank as "$0" or empty — triggers 5.D and Buyer forfeits termination right (common buyer-agent error).
  4. Confusing the optional period (5.B days) with the financing-contingency period (TPF Addendum ¶2 days) — these are distinct clocks.
  5. Pre-2021 muscle memory: agents still telling buyers to deliver the option fee directly to Seller. Per the 2021 amendment, option fee now goes to ESCROW AGENT (5.A). Direct delivery to Seller may still satisfy contract intent under 5.A(4) authorization-to-release, but the cleanest practice is escrow delivery.
- **Cross-reference.** ¶15 Default invoked by 5.C failure to deliver. Page 11 OPTION FEE RECEIPT (escrow receipt block 1) acknowledges receipt. Page 11 EARNEST MONEY RECEIPT (block 2) and ADDITIONAL EARNEST MONEY RECEIPT (block 4) acknowledge those receipts. ¶22 has the various financing addenda whose own timelines start from Effective Date.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `earnest_money_escrow_agent_name`, `earnest_money_escrow_agent_address`, `earnest_money_amount` (5.A initial earnest money $), `option_fee_amount` (5.A Option Fee $), `additional_earnest_money_amount` (5.A(1) $), `additional_earnest_money_days` (5.A(1) days), `option_period_days` (5.B days blank). **5.A(2) is fixed text — no fill.** Page-11 receipt blocks have their own role-prefixed keys: `option_fee_receipt_*`, `earnest_money_receipt_*`, `contract_receipt_*`, `additional_earnest_money_receipt_*` (Hadley convention #4).

### ¶6 TITLE POLICY AND SURVEY

- **Legal function.** Allocates responsibility for title insurance (Owner's Policy), establishes the title-commitment + objection process (the contractual machinery for clearing title), allocates survey responsibility, and delivers statutory disclosures piggybacking on the contract (POA membership, statutory tax districts, tide waters, annexation, certificated service area, public improvement districts, transfer fees, propane service area, water-level fluctuations, mold remediation, and other required notices).
- **Authority source.** Texas Insurance Code Chapter 2703 (Title Insurance — Procedural Rules) governs the title policy form. Texas Department of Insurance procedural rules govern T-47 / T-47.1 affidavits. Texas Property Code §5.012 (POA membership notice), §5.013 (statutory tax district notice), §5.014 (PID notice), §5.205 (transfer fees), §33.135 Natural Resources Code (tide waters), §13.257 Water Code (certificated service area), §141.010 Utilities Code (propane gas service area), §11 Water Code (water-level fluctuations), §1958.154 Occupations Code (mold remediation certificates).
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **6.A TITLE POLICY.** Seller furnishes Buyer Owner's Policy at ☐ Seller's / ☐ Buyer's expense issued by ____ (Title Company), in the amount of the Sales Price, dated at or after closing, insuring against loss subject to provisions of Title Policy (including existing building/zoning ordinances) and nine numbered exceptions: (1) restrictive covenants common to platted subdivision; (2) standard printed exception for standby fees, taxes, assessments; (3) liens created as part of financing in ¶3; (4) utility easements created by dedication deed/plat; (5) reservations/exceptions permitted by contract or approved by Buyer in writing; (6) standard printed exception as to marital rights; (7) standard printed exception as to waters/tidelands/beaches/streams/related matters; (8) standard printed exception as to discrepancies, conflicts, shortages in area or boundary lines, encroachments/protrusions, or overlapping improvements — with paired (i) "will not be amended or deleted from the title policy" / (ii) "will be amended to read 'shortages in area' at the expense of ☐ Buyer / ☐ Seller"; (9) exception/exclusion regarding minerals approved by TDI.
  - **6.B COMMITMENT.** Within 20 days after Title Company receives a copy of this contract, Seller shall furnish Buyer a commitment for title insurance (Commitment) and, at Buyer's expense, legible copies of restrictive covenants and exception documents (Exception Documents). Title Company delivers to Buyer at Buyer's address in ¶21. If not delivered within specified time, time for delivery automatically extended up to 15 days or 3 days before Closing Date, whichever is earlier. If not delivered within time required, Buyer may terminate and earnest money refunded.
  - **6.C SURVEY.** Survey by registered professional land surveyor acceptable to Title Company and Buyer's lender(s). **Check ONE box only.**
    - **(1)** Within ____ days after Effective Date, Seller furnishes Buyer + Title Company Seller's EXISTING survey AND a Residential Real Property Affidavit promulgated by Texas Department of Insurance (**T-47 Affidavit OR T-47.1 Declaration** — **2025-new option**). Buyer obtains new survey at Seller's expense no later than 3 days prior to Closing Date if Seller fails to furnish; if Title Company or Buyer's lender does not accept existing survey or affidavit/declaration, Buyer obtains new at ☐ Seller's / ☐ Buyer's expense no later than 3 days prior to Closing Date.
    - **(2)** Within ____ days after Effective Date, Buyer **MAY** obtain new survey at Buyer's expense. (**2025 change**: was "shall," now "may.") Buyer deemed to receive survey on date of actual receipt or date specified in paragraph, whichever earlier. **2025-new sentence**: If Buyer fails to obtain survey, Buyer may NOT terminate the contract under ¶2.B of the Third Party Financing Addendum because the survey was not obtained.
    - **(3)** Within ____ days after Effective Date, Seller at Seller's expense furnishes a new survey to Buyer.
  - **6.D OBJECTIONS.** Buyer may object to defects/exceptions/encumbrances to title (i) on the survey other than items 6.A(1)–(7), (ii) in the Commitment other than 6.A(1)–(9), or (iii) which prohibit the following use or activity: ____. Buyer must object by earlier of (i) Closing Date or (ii) ____ days after receipt of Commitment/Exception Documents/survey. Failure to timely object = waiver. Seller is not obligated to incur expense; may cure timely objections within 15 days after receipt of objections (Cure Period); Closing Date extended as necessary. If not cured within Cure Period, Buyer may within 5 days after end of Cure Period (i) terminate (EM refunded) or (ii) waive. Failure to terminate timely = deemed waiver.
  - **6.E TITLE NOTICES.**
    - **(1) ABSTRACT OR TITLE POLICY.** Broker advisory.
    - **(2) MEMBERSHIP IN PROPERTY OWNERS ASSOCIATION(S).** Paired ☐ "is" / ☐ "is not" subject to mandatory membership in POA; if subject, statutory POA disclosure language under §5.012 Texas Property Code follows (assessments, foreclosure risk, §207.003 right to resale certificate, recommendation to use TREC POA Addendum 36-11).
    - **(3) STATUTORY TAX DISTRICTS.** §5.013 / Ch. 49 Water Code notice if in a utility/water/sewer/drainage/flood control district.
    - **(4) TIDE WATERS.** §33.135 Natural Resources Code coastal notice if tidally influenced.
    - **(5) ANNEXATION.** §5.011 Texas Property Code ETJ notice if outside municipality.
    - **(6) CERTIFICATED SERVICE AREA OF UTILITY PROVIDER.** §13.257 Water Code notice.
    - **(7) PUBLIC IMPROVEMENT DISTRICTS.** §5.014 Property Code notice; addendum attached if in PID.
    - **(8) TRANSFER FEES.** §5.205 Property Code notice (private transfer fee obligations under §5 Subchapter G).
    - **(9) PROPANE GAS SYSTEM SERVICE AREA.** §141.010 Texas Utilities Code notice.
    - **(10) NOTICE OF WATER LEVEL FLUCTUATIONS.** Ch. 11 Water Code reservoir/lake notice if Property adjoins impoundment of ≥5,000 acre-feet.
    - **(11) CERTIFICATE OF MOLD REMEDIATION.** **2025-new paragraph.** "If the Property has been remediated for mold, Seller must provide to Buyer each certificate of mold damage remediation issued under §1958.154, Occupations Code, during the 5 years preceding the sale of the Property." (Tex. Occ. Code §1958.154 itself requires the certificate be transferred at sale.)
    - **(12) REQUIRED NOTICES.** Free-text: "The following notices have been given or are attached to this contract (for example, utility, water, drainage, and public improvement districts): ____." 2025 added language: "Seller's failure to provide applicable statutory notices may provide Buyer with remedies or rights to terminate the contract."
- **Who fills it, when.** Title-policy expense allocation (6.A): Seller's agent typically defaults to Seller-pays in Texas, though negotiated. Title Company name: Buyer-side selection in many TX markets, but contract-allowable for either party to name. Survey 6.C box selection: depends on whether Seller has existing survey (most common = 6.C(1)). 6.D objection-days: typically 10–20 days after Commitment receipt. 6.E.(2) POA paired checkbox: filled by listing agent based on Seller's disclosure. 6.E.(11) mold-cert: triggered only if Seller has had mold remediation in past 5 years.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Leaving the 6.A title-expense checkbox blank — common ambiguity that escrow has to chase down post-execution.
  2. Pre-2025 muscle memory on 6.C(1): assuming the T-47 Affidavit is mandatory. **As of 2025**, T-47.1 Declaration (non-notarized) is permitted as an alternative. Source: TREC, "Form Changes Effective January 3, 2025"; Republic Title, "2025 TREC Contract Changes" (https://www.republictitle.com/2025-trec-contract-changes-important-things-you-need-to-know-highlights-of-the-changes/).
  3. Failing to recognize that 6.C(2) is now "may" not "shall" — if Buyer does NOT obtain the survey, Buyer FORFEITS the termination right under TPF Addendum ¶2.B because of no survey. Big trap for buyer agents who assume they can fall back on TPF for a survey-related out.
  4. Not checking 6.E(2) HOA "is subject" box AND not attaching TREC 36-11 — leaves no operative POA addendum and Buyer's resale certificate rights are unclear. **Note: 36-11 updated effective May 28, 2026 per Hadley's INDEX.md note.**
  5. Forgetting 6.E.(11) mold cert disclosure when Seller has a prior remediation cert. §1958.154 Tex. Occ. Code imposes the duty independently — failure to deliver the cert is a §1958.154 violation regardless of contract.
  6. Treating 6.E.(12) as a throwaway free-text. The "Seller's failure to provide applicable statutory notices may provide Buyer with remedies or rights to terminate" language is a 2025 addition with TEETH.
  7. Missing the 5-day post-Cure-Period window in 6.D — Buyer must terminate (i) or waive (ii) within 5 days or is DEEMED to have waived.
- **Cross-reference.** 6.E.(2) HOA box ⇒ TREC 36-11 (Addendum for Property Subject to Mandatory Membership in POA). 6.E.(4) tidewaters ⇒ TREC coastal-area addendum. 6.E.(7) PID ⇒ "Addendum containing Notice of Obligation to Pay Improvement District Assessment" (¶22). 6.E.(9) propane ⇒ TREC Addendum for Property in a Propane Gas System Service Area. 6.C(2) survey termination ties to TPF Addendum 40-11 ¶2.B.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `title_policy_expense_paid_by` (paired Seller/Buyer in 6.A — engine inverts on a single boolean: true=Seller, false=Buyer — OR mutually-exclusive two-key pair, depending on label-engine convention; see Hadley's existing label file for the choice in effect); `title_company_name`; `title_policy_8_amendment` (paired (i)/(ii) Y/N — same key, engine inverts) and `title_policy_8_amendment_expense_paid_by` (paired Buyer/Seller in 8.(ii)); `commitment_days` (6.B — fixed at 20 days in form text, NOT a fill blank in 6.B itself though some practitioners read it that way); `survey_option` (6.C 3-way: `existing` / `buyer_new` / `seller_new`); `survey_days` (the days blank in whichever 6.C sub-option selected); `survey_alt_expense_paid_by` (6.C(1) ☐ Seller's / ☐ Buyer's expense for new survey if existing not accepted); `title_objections_use_activity` (6.D free-text); `title_objections_cure_days` (6.D days blank); `hoa_subject_to_mandatory_membership` (paired Y/N in 6.E(2)); `required_notices_listed` (6.E(12) free-text).

### ¶7 PROPERTY CONDITION

- **Legal function.** Allocates inspection rights and the consequences of property condition between contract and closing. Surfaces the Seller's Disclosure Notice (SDN) duty under §5.008 Texas Property Code, the federal Lead-Based Paint Disclosure duty (24 CFR Part 35 Subpart A; 42 U.S.C. §4852d), and ties to optional environmental and residential service contract (home warranty) elections.
- **Authority source.** Texas Property Code §5.008 (Seller's Disclosure of Property Condition — the source-of-truth statute for the SDN form / OP-H). 24 CFR Part 35 Subpart A (Residential Lead-Based Paint Hazard Reduction Act of 1992 implementing regs). Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation rules for inspectors (Tex. Occ. Code Ch. 1102).
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **7.A ACCESS, INSPECTIONS, UTILITIES.** Seller permits Buyer + Buyer's agents reasonable access. Buyer may have Property inspected by inspectors selected by Buyer and licensed by TREC or otherwise permitted by law. Any hydrostatic testing must be separately authorized by Seller in writing. Seller at Seller's expense shall cause existing utilities to be turned on and keep them on during contract.
  - **7.B SELLER'S DISCLOSURE NOTICE PURSUANT TO §5.008, TEXAS PROPERTY CODE (Notice).** **Check ONE box only.**
    - **(1)** Buyer has received the Notice.
    - **(2)** Buyer has not received the Notice. Within ____ days after Effective Date of this contract, Seller shall deliver the Notice to Buyer. If Buyer does not receive the Notice, Buyer may terminate this contract at any time prior to the closing and the earnest money will be refunded to Buyer. If Seller delivers the Notice, Buyer may terminate this contract for any reason within 7 days after Buyer receives the Notice or prior to the closing, whichever first occurs, and the earnest money will be refunded to Buyer.
    - **(3)** The Seller is not required to furnish the notice under the Texas Property Code.
  - **7.C SELLER'S DISCLOSURE OF LEAD-BASED PAINT AND LEAD-BASED PAINT HAZARDS** required by Federal law for residential dwelling constructed prior to 1978.
  - **7.D ACCEPTANCE OF PROPERTY CONDITION.** "As Is" means present condition with any and all defects and without warranty except for warranties of title and warranties in this contract. Buyer's agreement to accept "As Is" under 7.D(1) or 7.D(2) does NOT preclude inspecting under 7.A, negotiating repairs in a subsequent amendment, or terminating during Option Period. **Check ONE box only.**
    - **(1)** Buyer accepts the Property As Is.
    - **(2)** Buyer accepts the Property As Is provided Seller, at Seller's expense, shall complete the following specific repairs and treatments: ____. (Do NOT insert general phrases such as "subject to inspections" that do not identify specific repairs and treatments.)
  - **7.E LENDER REQUIRED REPAIRS AND TREATMENTS.** Unless otherwise agreed, neither party obligated to pay for lender-required repairs (includes wood-destroying-insect treatment). If parties don't agree, contract terminates and earnest money refunded. If cost exceeds 5% of Sales Price, Buyer may terminate and earnest money refunded.
  - **7.F COMPLETION OF REPAIRS AND TREATMENTS.** Unless otherwise agreed, Seller shall complete all agreed repairs before Closing Date, obtain required permits. Repairs must be performed by licensed persons (or commercially engaged in the trade if no license required). Seller shall: (i) provide Buyer documentation showing scope of work and payment for completed work; (ii) at Seller's expense, transfer transferable warranties to Buyer at closing. If Seller fails to complete, Buyer may exercise ¶15 remedies OR extend Closing Date up to 5 days.
  - **7.G ENVIRONMENTAL MATTERS.** Buyer advised re wetlands, toxic substances (asbestos, wastes, other environmental hazards), threatened/endangered species. If Buyer is concerned, addendum promulgated by TREC or required by parties should be used.
  - **7.H RESIDENTIAL SERVICE CONTRACTS.** Buyer may purchase home warranty from provider licensed by Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation. If Buyer purchases, Seller shall reimburse Buyer at closing for cost not exceeding $____. **Purchase of a residential service contract is optional.**
- **Who fills it, when.** 7.B box: Listing agent fills based on whether Seller has delivered the SDN before offer. 7.B(2) days blank: typically 3–10 days. 7.D As-Is checkbox: Buyer's election in the offer. 7.D(2) repairs blank: Buyer's agent lists specific items if negotiated up-front. 7.H dollar cap: Buyer's agent if buyer wants home warranty reimbursed.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Checking 7.B(3) "Seller is not required to furnish" without confirming an actual §5.008 exemption applies. §5.008(e) lists 11 exemptions (estate sale, foreclosure, transfer between spouses, etc.). Wrong (3)-checking = §5.008(d) violation + statutory damages. Source: Texas REALTORS, "Disclosure Requirements" (https://www.texasrealestate.com/members/legal-and-ethics/resources/legal-faq/disclosure-requirements/).
  2. Writing "subject to inspections" or "TBD" in 7.D(2) repairs blank — the form expressly prohibits general phrases. Industry-standard fix: leave 7.D(2) blank at offer, then amend via TREC 39-9 after inspection.
  3. Forgetting OP-L Lead-Based Paint addendum when property built before 1978. **This is a FEDERAL requirement** (24 CFR 35.92 et seq.), not TREC-discretionary. Penalties under 42 USC §4852d include up to 3× damages.
  4. Mis-checking the As-Is box: 7.D(1) "As Is" with no negotiated repairs ≠ no right to inspect. Buyer retains 7.A inspection right and Option Period termination right. Common buyer-agent overreach: assuming As-Is means walking away from inspection.
  5. Treating 7.H residential service contract reimbursement cap as if it's a separate addendum — it's a contract term right here in ¶7. The cap goes in the dollar blank in this paragraph; no separate addendum needed.
- **Cross-reference.** OP-H Seller's Disclosure Notice form is the deliverable referenced in 7.B (separate document, NOT a contract addendum — required for resale property older than 1 year per §5.008). OP-L Lead-Based Paint Addendum attaches via ¶22 checkbox when property pre-1978. ¶15 default remedies invoked by 7.F repair-completion failure. Environmental addendum in 7.G is the Environmental Assessment, Threatened or Endangered Species and Wetlands Addendum (¶22 checkbox).
- **Fixture key conventions.** `sdn_received_status` (7.B 3-way: `received` / `not_received` / `not_required` — separate keys per Hadley convention #2: `sdn_received` / `sdn_not_received` / `sdn_not_required` mutually-exclusive); `sdn_delivery_days` (7.B(2) days blank); `as_is_repairs_specific` (7.D 2-way paired: `as_is_no_repairs` / `as_is_with_repairs` — separate keys or paired Y/N depending on engine convention; current Hadley label file should be checked); `as_is_repairs_list` (7.D(2) free-text); `residential_service_contract_cap` (7.H $ blank).

### ¶8 BROKERS AND SALES AGENTS

- **Legal function.** Surfaces required disclosures of broker/agent self-interest in the transaction (familial, ownership, fiduciary), and confirms that the parties' brokerage-fee agreements live in SEPARATE written agreements (the Listing Agreement and Buyer Representation Agreement), NOT in this contract.
- **Authority source.** TRELA §1101.652(b)(5) requires written disclosure of license-holder's interest. 22 TAC §535.144 (license holder's own/family interest). Post-NAR-Settlement (2024) and the resulting written-buyer-rep-agreement requirement codified in 22 TAC §535.158 (effective for buyer transactions).
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **8.A BROKER OR SALES AGENT DISCLOSURE.** "Texas law requires a real estate broker or sales agent who is a party to a transaction or acting on behalf of a spouse, parent, child, business entity in which the broker or sales agent owns more than 10%, or a trust for which the broker or sales agent acts as a trustee or of which the broker or sales agent or the broker or sales agent's spouse, parent or child is a beneficiary, to notify the other party in writing before entering into a contract of sale. **Disclose if applicable:** ____."
  - **8.B BROKERS' FEES.** "All obligations of the parties for payment of brokers' fees are contained in separate written agreements." (Per the post-NAR-settlement BLC commentary, this paragraph reinforces that broker fees are negotiable and not set by law — TREC's Jan 2025 change summary; freedom-res.com summary at https://www.freedom-res.com/posts/key-updates-to-texas-real-estate-forms-what-agents-need-to-know-for-2024/2025/.)
- **Who fills it, when.** 8.A free-text filled ONLY when disclosable interest exists. Most transactions: 8.A blank or "N/A." Time: contract drafting if interest present.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Skipping 8.A when the agent is a relative of one party — TRELA §1101.652(b)(5) violation. License discipline risk.
  2. Trying to write the buyer-broker compensation into 8.B — broker compensation goes in the SEPARATE buyer rep agreement, plus the Seller-contribution-to-buyer-broker-fees option in ¶12.A.(1)(b). 8.B is fixed text, NOT a fill blank.
- **Cross-reference.** ¶12.A.(1)(b) is where Seller-paid Buyer brokerage contribution lives now (post-2025). Page 10 broker information block separately identifies the agents/brokers and the role each plays.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `broker_agent_disclosure` (8.A free-text). 8.B has no fill fields.

### ¶9 CLOSING

- **Legal function.** Fixes the Closing Date and specifies the documents and acts required at closing.
- **Authority source.** Common-law contract performance; TX Property Code conveyance requirements (Ch. 5, esp. §5.022 general warranty deed form recital).
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **9.A** "The closing of the sale will be on or before ____, 20____, or within 7 days after objections made under ¶6.D have been cured or waived, whichever date is later (Closing Date). If either party fails to close the sale by the Closing Date, the non-defaulting party may exercise the remedies contained in ¶15."
  - **9.B** AT CLOSING: (1) Seller delivers GENERAL WARRANTY DEED + tax statements/certificates showing no delinquent taxes; (2) Buyer pays Sales Price in good funds acceptable to Escrow Agent; (3) parties execute notices/statements/affidavits/releases/loan documents/transfer of warranties/other documents reasonably required; (4) no liens/assessments/security interests against Property unless assumed; (5) private transfer fees obligation of Seller unless otherwise provided (governed by HOA Addendum 36-11).
- **Who fills it, when.** Closing Date: Buyer's agent in the initial offer; often counter-negotiated. Drafted at contract creation.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Picking a Closing Date too tight on financing (lender typically needs 30–45 days for new conventional loan).
  2. Assuming verbal closing-date extensions are binding — they are NOT. Must be amended via TREC 39-9.
  3. Missing the automatic 7-day Closing Date extension when ¶6.D objections are still in their cure period — this is automatic per 9.A, no amendment needed. (And 7.F gives an automatic up-to-5-day extension if repairs aren't done.)
- **Cross-reference.** ¶6.D cure-period extension feeds 9.A. ¶7.F repair-completion-failure extends 9.A by up to 5 days. ¶15 invoked for failure to close. Loan-side dates in TPF Addendum 40-11 may be linked to Closing Date.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `closing_date` (9.A date — could be `closing_month_day` + `closing_year_yy` if split widgets, OR a single date string; verify against PDF widget layout).

### ¶10 POSSESSION

- **Legal function.** Establishes when Buyer takes physical possession (default = at closing/funding), and the legal status of any pre-closing or post-closing occupancy. Triggers the temporary-lease addenda (TREC 16-6 buyer's temp lease, 15-6 seller's temp lease).
- **Authority source.** Common-law possession doctrine; Texas Property Code Ch. 92 (residential landlord/tenant — relevant if temp lease used). The "tenancy at sufferance" warning is rooted in common-law landlord-tenant law.
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **10.A BUYER'S POSSESSION.** Seller shall deliver Buyer possession in present condition, ordinary wear-and-tear excepted: ☐ upon closing and funding; ☐ according to a temporary residential lease form promulgated by TREC or other written lease required by the parties. Any possession by Buyer prior to closing or by Seller after closing which is not authorized by a written lease will establish a tenancy at sufferance. Insurance-coverage warning.
  - **10.B SMART DEVICES.** "Smart Device" means a device that connects to the internet to enable remote use, monitoring, and management of: (i) the Property; (ii) items identified in any Non-Realty Items Addendum; or (iii) items in a Fixture Lease assigned to Buyer. At time Seller delivers possession to Buyer, Seller shall: (1) deliver to Buyer written information containing all access codes, usernames, passwords, and applications Buyer will need to access, operate, manage, and control the Smart Devices; and (2) terminate and remove all access and connections to the improvements and accessories from any of Seller's personal devices including but not limited to phones and computers.
- **Who fills it, when.** Listing agent typically defaults to "upon closing and funding" unless Seller needs leaseback time. If Seller staying post-closing → check temp-lease box + attach TREC 15-6. If Buyer needs early possession → check temp-lease box + attach TREC 16-6.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Allowing Seller to stay post-closing without a written temp lease — creates tenancy at sufferance, complicates eviction, insurance gap. The form's bold warning is explicit.
  2. Forgetting 10.B smart-device password handoff at funding. Common Buyer complaint post-closing: "I can't get into the smart locks / thermostat / ring cameras."
  3. Checking the temp-lease box but NOT attaching the actual TREC 15-6 or 16-6 — leaves no operative lease terms.
- **Cross-reference.** Temp-lease box triggers TREC 16-6 (Buyer's Temporary Residential Lease) or 15-6 (Seller's Temporary Residential Lease) attached via ¶22. ¶2.C "Controls" definition ties to 10.B smart-device handoff.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `possession_timing` (10.A paired Y/N: `possession_at_closing` vs `possession_temp_lease` — paired Y/N convention, same key, engine inverts).

### ¶11 SPECIAL PROVISIONS

- **Legal function.** Free-text blank for "additional informational items" only. The contract paragraph itself states: "An informational item is a statement that completes a blank in a contract form, discloses factual information, or provides instructions. Real estate brokers and sales agents are prohibited from practicing law and shall not add to, delete, or modify any provision of this contract unless drafted by a party to this contract or a party's attorney."
- **Authority source.** **22 TAC §537.11(b)(5)** prohibits a license holder from drafting or recommending language defining or affecting rights/obligations/remedies. **§537.11(b)(6)** prohibits adding informational items to a TREC-promulgated form if the Commission has approved another form for that purpose. Violation = unauthorized practice of law under TRELA §1101.654 + Tex. Penal Code §38.123. Source: 22 TAC §537.11 (https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/texas/22-Tex-Admin-Code-SS-537-11).
- **Who fills it, when.** A PARTY or a PARTY'S ATTORNEY drafts substantive language; a license holder may type factual/informational items only. Time: contract drafting.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.** (This paragraph is THE most-cited UPL trap in Texas residential practice. MetroTex: https://www.mymetrotex.com/whats-the-purpose-of-paragraph-11-special-provisions-and-what-can-i-write/; Spielvogel, "Special Provisions — Paragraph 11" https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/special-provisions-paragraph-11-paul-spielvogel.)
  1. **Adding "subject to sale of Buyer's other property" without using the TREC Addendum for Sale of Other Property by Buyer (the ¶22 checkbox).** TREC has a promulgated form for this; using ¶11 instead violates §537.11(b)(6).
  2. **Adding kick-out clauses, escalation clauses, appraisal contingencies.** TREC has promulgated 49-1 for appraisal termination — using ¶11 to handcraft an appraisal-out is UPL.
  3. **Adding "as is per inspection" / "subject to inspection" / "to be determined" placeholder language** — these are legal-effect modifiers, not factual statements, and violate §537.11(b)(5).
  4. **Adding "time of essence" language** — already in the contract.
  5. **Pasting in lender pre-approval language.** Lender requirements go in 40-11 TPF Addendum, not ¶11.
  6. **Listing inclusion of refrigerator / non-fixture appliances.** Cleanest path: typed addition to 2.C accessories or non-realty items addendum. ¶11 is a poor venue.
- **Cross-reference.** Virtually every "special provision" idea has an existing TREC addendum that should be used instead — see ¶22 checklist.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `special_provisions` (¶11 multi-line free-text). The fill engine should refuse to populate this with non-factual content from Dossie's AI — defer to human/attorney drafting.

### ¶12 SETTLEMENT AND OTHER EXPENSES

- **Legal function.** Allocates closing costs between Seller and Buyer, including (post-2025) the new ability for Seller to contribute toward Buyer's brokerage fees and other Buyer expenses. Implements the post-NAR-Settlement compensation transparency framework.
- **Authority source.** Contract freedom; no specific TREC rule mandates the cost allocation, but 22 TAC §535.158 (post-NAR settlement written buyer-rep requirement) and §535.144 frame how broker compensation is now disclosed. The 2025 12.A restructure was driven by the November 2024 BLC recommendation.
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **12.A** Expenses paid at or prior to closing:
    - **(1) SELLER'S EXPENSES.**
      - **(a)** Releases of existing liens (including prepayment penalties + recording fees); release of Seller's loan liability; tax statements/certificates; preparation of deed; one-half of escrow fee; brokerage fees that Seller has agreed to pay; and other expenses payable by Seller under this contract.
      - **(b)** **2025-new:** "the following amount to be applied to brokerage fees that Buyer has agreed to pay: ☐ $____ or ☐ ____% of the Sales Price (check one box only)." This is the Seller-contribution-to-Buyer's-brokerage-fees field — replaces (in part) the prior MLS-cooperative compensation model after the NAR settlement.
      - **(c)** an amount not to exceed $____ to be applied to other Buyer's Expenses.
    - **(2) BUYER'S EXPENSES.** Appraisal fees; loan application fees; origination charges; credit reports; preparation of loan documents; interest on the notes from disbursement to one month prior to first monthly payment dates; recording fees; copies of easements/restrictions; loan title policy with required endorsements; loan-related inspection fees; photos; amortization schedules; one-half of escrow; all prepaid items (including required premiums for flood/hazard insurance, reserve deposits for insurance, ad valorem taxes, special governmental assessments); final compliance inspection; courier fee; repair inspection; underwriting fee; wire transfer fee; expenses incident to loan; PMI / VA Loan Funding Fee / FHA MIP; brokerage fees that Buyer has agreed to pay; other Buyer-payable expenses.
  - **12.B** If any expense exceeds amount expressly stated for a party to pay, that party may terminate UNLESS the other party agrees to pay the excess. **Buyer may not pay charges/fees expressly prohibited by FHA, VA, Texas Veterans Land Board, or other governmental loan program regulations.**
- **Who fills it, when.** 12.A.(1)(b) Seller-contribution dollar/percent: Listing agent fills based on what was negotiated in the listing agreement OR as concession in offer/counter. 12.A.(1)(c) "other Buyer's Expenses" cap: traditionally where Seller-paid closing costs concessions go (e.g., $5,000 toward Buyer closing costs). Time: contract drafting.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Confusing 12.A.(1)(b) (broker fees) with 12.A.(1)(c) (other Buyer expenses). The two are distinct: (b) is specifically for Buyer's brokerage fees; (c) is everything else.
  2. Checking BOTH ☐ $ and ☐ % in 12.A.(1)(b) — the form expressly says "check one box only."
  3. Leaving 12.A.(1)(b) blank when the listing offered cooperative compensation — Seller may be on the hook for Buyer's broker fee per separate buyer rep agreement, but 12.A.(1)(b) is the contractual vehicle to apply it at closing.
  4. Putting Seller-paid Buyer closing-cost concessions in ¶11 special provisions instead of 12.A.(1)(c) — UPL risk under §537.11(b)(6).
  5. Forgetting the FHA/VA/TVLB/governmental-loan prohibition on certain Buyer-paid fees in 12.B — Buyer-paid fees that violate program rules will be flipped to Seller-paid at closing, surprising the Seller. Source: Texas REALTORS, "How to Use Paragraph 12 and Broker Compensation Agreements" (updated 01-23-25; https://www.texasrealestate.com/wp-content/uploads/Paragraph12AndBrokerCompensationAgreements-1.pdf).
- **Cross-reference.** ¶8.B (broker fees in separate written agreements) tied to 12.A.(1)(b). Page 10 inter-broker compensation disclosure tied to 12.A.(1)(b).
- **Fixture key conventions.** `seller_contribution_to_buyer_broker_fees_amount` (12.A.(1)(b) $ blank), `seller_contribution_to_buyer_broker_fees_percent` (12.A.(1)(b) % blank), `seller_contribution_to_buyer_broker_fees_basis` (paired Y/N: same key, engine inverts: true=$ checked, false=% checked); `seller_contribution_other_buyer_expenses_cap` (12.A.(1)(c) $ blank).

### ¶13 PRORATIONS

### ¶15 DEFAULT

- **Legal function.** Specifies the menu of remedies if either party breaches.
- **Authority source.** Common-law contract remedies; doctrine of liquidated damages (must be reasonable estimate of damages, not punitive — Phillips v. Phillips, 820 S.W.2d 785 (Tex. 1991)); doctrine of specific performance for unique property.
- **Sub-paragraph.** **Buyer default:** Seller may (a) enforce specific performance, seek such other relief as may be provided by law, or both, or (b) terminate this contract and receive the earnest money as liquidated damages, thereby releasing both parties. **Seller default:** Buyer may (a) enforce specific performance, seek such other relief as may be provided by law, or both, or (b) terminate this contract and receive the earnest money, thereby releasing both parties.
- **Who fills it, when.** No fill fields. Triggered by breach.

### ¶18 ESCROW

- **Legal function.** Defines Escrow Agent's role, liability limits, expense handling, and the demand-for-release mechanism for earnest money on contract termination.
- **Authority source.** Texas Finance Code Ch. 651 (escrow agent regulation by TDI); 22 TAC §537.28 form promulgation.
- **Sub-paragraphs.**
  - **18.A ESCROW.** Escrow Agent is NOT (i) a party, (ii) liable for performance/nonperformance of any party, (iii) liable for interest on earnest money, or (iv) liable for loss of earnest money caused by failure of financial institution holding it (unless financial institution is the Escrow Agent). May require any disbursement be conditioned on collection of good funds.
  - **18.B EXPENSES.** At closing, earnest money applied first to cash down payment, then to Buyer's Expenses, excess refunded to Buyer. If no closing: Escrow Agent may (i) require written release of liability before releasing earnest money; (ii) require payment of unpaid expenses incurred on a party's behalf, may deduct authorized expenses.
  - **18.C DEMAND.** Upon termination, either party or Escrow Agent may send a release of earnest money to each party. If a party makes written demand for earnest money, Escrow Agent shall promptly provide a copy of demand to the other party. **If Escrow Agent does not receive written objection from other party within 15 days, Escrow Agent may disburse the earnest money** to the party making demand reduced by unpaid expenses incurred on behalf of the party receiving earnest money. Escrow Agent may pay same to creditors. Compliance releases Escrow Agent from claims related to disbursal.
  - **18.D DAMAGES.** Any party who wrongfully fails or refuses to sign a release acceptable to Escrow Agent within 7 days of receipt of request is liable to the other party for (i) damages; (ii) the earnest money; (iii) reasonable attorney's fees; (iv) all costs of suit.
  - **18.E NOTICES.** Escrow Agent's notices effective when sent in compliance with ¶21. Notice of objection to demand is effective on receipt by Escrow Agent.
- **Who fills it, when.** No fill fields. Boilerplate.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Buyer/Seller refusing to sign earnest-money release out of spite — exposes to 18.D damages + fees.
  2. Failing to register a written objection within the 15-day window — Escrow Agent will release per 18.C, and complaining party loses the funds.
  3. Treating Escrow Agent as a decision-maker on who's "right" — Escrow Agent is a stakeholder, not arbiter.
- **Cross-reference.** ¶15 default precedes earnest-money demand. ¶21 governs notice mechanics for 18.C demand.
- **Fixture key conventions.** No fill fields.

### ¶20 FEDERAL REQUIREMENTS

- **Legal function.** Withholding obligation under FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) when Seller is a "foreign person."
- **Authority source.** 26 U.S.C. §1445; 26 CFR §1.1445; IRS Form 8288 / 8288-A / 8288-B.
- **Sub-paragraph.** "If Seller is a 'foreign person,' as defined by Internal Revenue Code and its regulations, or if Seller fails to deliver an affidavit or a certificate of non-foreign status to Buyer that Seller is not a 'foreign person,' then Buyer shall withhold from the sales proceeds an amount sufficient to comply with applicable tax law and deliver the same to the Internal Revenue Service together with appropriate tax forms. - **Legal function.** Specifies notice mechanics and delivery addresses controlling for ALL contract notices (termination notices, objection notices, demand letters, etc.). The "delivered to" of any address-effective clock under the contract.
- **Authority source.** Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §322 (Texas Uniform Electronic Transactions Act — recognizes electronic notice); common law on notice delivery.
- **Sub-paragraph.** "All notices from one party to the other must be in writing and are effective when mailed to, hand-delivered at, or transmitted by fax or electronic transmission as follows:" then paired "To Buyer at:" / "To Seller at:" blocks each with address (multi-line), Phone (area-code split), E-mail/Fax, E-mail/Fax (a SECOND email/fax line per the 2025 update), "With a copy to Buyer's agent at:" / "With a copy to Seller's agent at:".

### ¶24 EXECUTION (page 9)

- **Legal function.** Captures the Effective Date (the date final acceptance is communicated to the other party — start-clock for all "X days after Effective Date" timers in the contract) and the parties' signatures.
- **Authority source.** Common law of contract formation (offer + acceptance + communication of acceptance); Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §322 (UETA) for e-signatures.
- **Sub-paragraph.** "EXECUTED the ____ day of ____, 20____ (Effective Date). (BROKER: FILL IN THE DATE OF FINAL ACCEPTANCE.)" Followed by two Buyer signature lines + two Seller signature lines.
- **Who fills it, when.** The BROKER (not party) fills the Effective Date based on when final acceptance was communicated. Parties sign in counterpart per common practice.
- **Common practitioner mistakes.**
  1. Treating "executed" date as the date the last signature was AFFIXED rather than the date final acceptance was COMMUNICATED to the offeror. These can differ — and the difference can shift every clock in the contract by a day or more.

- **Cross-reference.** Every "X days after Effective Date" clause anchors here.
- **Fixture key conventions.** `execution_day` (day-of-month numeric), `execution_month` (month text), `execution_year_yy` (year 2-digit). Engine should compute composite Effective Date string and feed every other paragraph's days-clock from this anchor.

## Common disputes / litigation patterns

1. **Earnest-money release standoffs (¶5 / ¶18).** Highest-frequency civil disputes. Pattern: Buyer terminates outside option period claiming Seller breach; Seller refuses to sign release; Escrow Agent holds funds. 18.C 15-day no-objection mechanism is the practical resolver. 18.D's "wrongful refusal to sign release" claim adds attorney's fees teeth.
2. **Failure-to-disclose SDN issues (¶7.B / §5.008).** Buyers sue post-closing for misrepresentation or omitted condition (foundation, plumbing, prior flooding, lead paint, mold). §5.008(f) liability survives closing. ¶19 reinforces survival.
3. **Repair-completion disputes (¶7.D(2) and ¶7.F).** Seller's specified repairs not done or done poorly. Buyer's election: ¶15 default OR up-to-5-day Closing Date extension.
4. **HOA assessment-and-resale-cert disputes (¶6.E(2) / 36-11).** Seller didn't deliver resale certificate; outstanding assessments surface post-closing.
5. **Survey-acceptance disputes (¶6.C).** Title Company or lender rejects Seller's existing survey + T-47/T-47.1; who pays for new survey turns on the (1) (i)/(ii) sub-checkboxes. Buyer may forfeit TPF Addendum ¶2.B termination right if Buyer fails to obtain survey in 6.C(2) scenario.
6. **Solar-panel lease assumption surprises (¶4.B).** Fixture lease not flagged at offer; Buyer's lender requires UCC-3 termination or lease assumption pre-closing; closing delayed/canceled.
7. **Wrongful retention of earnest money — §15 + §18 interplay.** Seller refuses to release after Buyer's timely option-period termination claiming Buyer didn't pay the option fee on time (5.D). Resolution turns on documentary proof of timely option-fee delivery (see page-11 OPTION FEE RECEIPT).
8. **UPL claims arising from ¶11 Special Provisions.** Bar association or TREC complaints against license holders who drafted contingency or kick-out language.
9. **Foreign-Seller FIRPTA withholding failures (¶20).** Buyer fails to withhold; IRS pursues Buyer for 15% of gross sales price. Cleanest path: title company collects and remits.
10. **Page-10 broker-disclosure misrepresentations (TRELA §1101.558 / §1101.561).** Other Broker checked as Listing Broker's subagent when actually representing Buyer; or Selling Associate filled in straight-agency representation (no intermediary appointments). License discipline.

## Hadley's personal expert notes (compounding wisdom)

1. **The Effective Date is the source of truth.** Every important deadline in this contract — 3-day earnest money / option fee delivery, 20-day commitment, X-day option period, X-day survey, X-day objection, Closing Date — counts from the Effective Date that the BROKER fills in ¶24 on page 9. If the broker leaves it blank, every clock in the contract is unanchored. Heath's fill engine MUST refuse to mark a contract "complete" if Effective Date is missing.

2. **The 4 escrow events on page 11 are 4 separate legal acts, not one combined "earnest money" event.** Conflating them in the fixture-key namespace was Hadley failure mode #6. The Option Fee receipt in particular is the document-of-record that defeats a Seller's 5.D "you never paid the option fee" defense in a termination-rights dispute.

3. **The 5-party broker block on page 10 is where TRELA §1101.558 / §1101.561 agency-misrepresentation liability lives.** Mis-checking "Seller as Listing Broker's subagent" when the broker is actually a Buyer's agent is the single most common license-discipline trigger I've researched. The Selling Associate block exists ONLY for intermediary-with-appointments scenarios under §1101.561 — leave it blank in straight agency.

4. **¶11 Special Provisions is a UPL minefield. Defer, defer, defer.** The Dossie fill engine should refuse to generate any non-factual language for ¶11. Any "subject to," "contingent on," "kick-out," "appraisal contingency" language belongs in an ADDENDUM, not ¶11. If Dossie's AI feels the urge to draft a clause for ¶11, that's the signal to send the agent to TREC 49-1 or another addendum (or to their broker's lawyer).

5. **The 2025 amendments materially changed two Hadley-relevant things:**
   - ¶6.C(2) "Buyer may obtain a new survey" — buyer agents who pattern-match on the old "shall" language and ALSO check 6.C(2) thinking they retain TPF Addendum ¶2.B termination rights are wrong. If Buyer doesn't get the survey, the TPF survey termination is GONE per the 2025-new sentence in 6.C(2).
   - ¶12.A.(1)(b) Seller-contribution-to-Buyer-brokerage-fees with paired $/% — this is the post-NAR-settlement replacement for MLS-cooperative-compensation. The fill engine MUST surface this field in any transaction with separate buyer-side representation. A blank 12.A.(1)(b) is now a meaningful financial fact (= "Seller is not contributing anything toward Buyer's broker fee").

6. **§5.008 SDN (OP-H) exemptions are narrow — never check 7.B(3) without source.** §5.008(e) lists 11 statutory exemptions (estate sale, foreclosure, transfer between spouses, transfer to/from gov't, transfer of unimproved property, etc.). Don't let an agent check 7.B(3) on a vibes-basis. The §5.008(d) statutory liability includes actual damages + cost of fines. (Property Code text at https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._prop._code_section_5.008.)

7. **PRIOR-MAP CORRECTION (page 8 vs page 9 for ¶23).** The Hadley agent system-prompt widget map placed ¶23 (Consult an Attorney) on page 9. The actual TREC 20-18 PDF in `api/_assets/trec-20-18-raw.pdf` has ¶23 on **page 8**, immediately below ¶22. Page 9 is the Execution / Effective Date / signatures page. Hadley fill-engine label-file maintenance must mirror the live PDF, not the prior map. Re-verified 2026-06-20.

8. **The "natural resource lease" geothermal addition is more than cosmetic.** 2023 Texas legislation recognized property-owner ownership of subsurface geothermal energy. A property with an active geothermal lease (rare but growing) now has the same contractual disclosure pathway as oil/gas/wind/water leases. Watch the Permian + Texas Hill Country deep-geothermal pilot projects — this checkbox will start mattering more in the next 5 years.

9. **The "10% ownership" trigger in ¶8.A is a TREC license-discipline tripwire** — license holders disclosing their familial / ownership / fiduciary interest under ¶8.A is mandatory under TRELA §1101.652(b)(5). The Dossie fill engine should PROMPT the licensed agent ("Are you, your spouse, parent, child, or any entity you own >10% of, a party to this transaction?") before allowing the agent to sign off on a blank ¶8.A.

10. **Page 11 Receipt-block timing matters for the Hadley fill flow.** Dossie should NOT pre-populate the page-11 receipt blocks at the moment of fill — those are escrow-agent-contemporaneous acknowledgments. Pre-population can be construed as fabrication. The fill engine should leave page 11 BLANK on initial fill (or "TBD by Escrow Agent") and route the file to the escrow agent's electronic-signature flow for separate execution.
