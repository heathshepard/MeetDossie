## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules

### ¶A — Release of Seller's Liability on Loan to be Assumed
- Within a specified number of days after the Effective Date (fillable), Seller AND Buyer shall apply to the appropriate authority for release of Seller's liability:
  - (a) any conventional lender, OR
  - (b) VA and any lender whose loan has been guaranteed by VA, OR
  - (c) FHA and any lender whose loan has been insured by FHA.
- Both parties furnish all required information and documents. Joint-diligence obligation — neither party can sit idle.
- **If the release is NOT approved by Closing Date, check ONE box:**
  - (1) Contract TERMINATES; earnest money refunded to Buyer.
  - (2) Failure to obtain release approval will NOT delay closing (deal proceeds; Seller stays liable).

The paired boxes are mutually exclusive — pick one. Leaving both blank creates ambiguity; the default policy is unclear from the form face, so the parties must choose.

### ¶B — Restoration of Seller's Entitlement for VA Loan
- Within a specified number of days after the Effective Date (fillable — separate blank from ¶A), Seller AND Buyer shall apply for restoration of Seller's VA entitlement and furnish all VA-required information and documents.
- **If restoration is NOT approved by Closing Date, check ONE box:**
  - (1) Contract TERMINATES; earnest money refunded to Buyer.
  - (2) Failure to obtain restoration approval will NOT delay closing.

### NOTICE printed on the form (load-bearing)
- **VA will NOT restore Seller's VA entitlement unless BUYER (a) is a veteran, (b) has sufficient unused VA entitlement, and (c) is otherwise qualified.**
- If Seller desires restoration of VA entitlement, **both** ¶A AND ¶B "should be used" (Seller needs the release AND the restoration; they're separate approvals).

### Closing paragraphs (post-boxes)
- Seller pays the cost of securing the release AND the restoration. Non-negotiable per the form.
- Seller's deed will contain any loan assumption clause required by FHA, VA, or any lender. (Deed language is dictated by the underlying loan program.)

## Common Q&A a working TX agent would ask

**Q1. My seller doesn't understand why we need this — the buyer is assuming their loan, so aren't they off the hook?**
A. No. Assumption transfers the payment obligation TO the buyer, but the seller's name remains on the note UNTIL the lender formally releases the seller's liability. Without a release, if the buyer defaults years from now, the noteholder can still come after the seller. TREC 12-3 ¶A is the mechanism to request that release. (TREC 12-3 ¶A; cross-reference TREC 41-3 NOTICE TO SELLER.)

**Q2. What's VA entitlement, and why does restoration matter?**
A. VA guarantees a portion of a VA loan. When a veteran uses their entitlement to buy a home, that portion is "consumed" until they either pay off the loan or get it restored. If the seller sold using a VA loan assumed by a non-veteran buyer, the seller's entitlement stays consumed until the loan is paid — meaning the seller may not be able to use VA benefits on their next home. Restoration requires the assuming buyer to be a veteran with sufficient unused entitlement. (TREC 12-3 ¶B and printed NOTICE.)

**Q3. If the lender says "no" to a release, do we terminate?**
A. Only if ¶A(1) was checked. If ¶A(2) was checked, closing proceeds even without the release — the seller remains on the note. Which box you check should reflect how much the seller cares about being released. Most sellers care a lot; most 12-3s should have ¶A(1) selected. (TREC 12-3 ¶A(1) / ¶A(2).)

**Q4. Does 12-3 stand alone, or must 41-3 be attached first?**
A. In practice, 12-3 rides on top of 41-3 (Loan Assumption Addendum). 12-3 is meaningless without an underlying assumption. The trigger for 12-3 is buyer-assumes-loan (TREC 20-18 ¶3.B "Loan Assumption") plus seller-wants-release-and/or-VA-restoration. (TREC 41-3 ¶F NOTICE TO SELLER points sellers to this form.)

**Q5. Who pays for the release / restoration application fees?**
A. Seller. The form explicitly states "Seller shall pay the cost of securing the release and restoration." Non-negotiable per the promulgated form. If Seller wants Buyer to share the cost, the parties would have to add a special provision at TREC 20-18 ¶11 — and even then, TRELA §1101.654 restricts brokers/agents from drafting legal language there.

**Q6. Buyer isn't a veteran. Can we still use ¶B?**
A. No — VA restoration requires the buyer be a qualifying veteran with sufficient unused entitlement. If Buyer is a non-veteran, ¶B is inapplicable; use only ¶A to seek a lender release, but Seller's VA entitlement will remain consumed until the underlying loan is paid off (which may take decades).

**Q7. How long does a VA release/restoration typically take?**
A. Not codified in the form — TREC unverified. Practically, VA release + restoration processing runs anywhere from 30-90+ days depending on VA regional loan center workload. If closing is 30 days out, calendar this early and set ¶A / ¶B day-counts short (like 5-10 days) so applications go in immediately after Effective Date.

**Q8. Can we use 12-3 for an FHA release without touching VA sections?**
A. Yes. ¶A explicitly lists "FHA and any lender whose loan has been insured by FHA" as one of the release paths. Leave ¶B unused (Buyer initials suffice to indicate ¶B doesn't apply) — VA restoration is only relevant for VA loans. (TREC 12-3 ¶A(c).)

**Q9. What if only ¶A applies but not ¶B?**
A. Use ¶A alone. The form is designed to be modular — ¶A can apply without ¶B, and vice versa. The boxes and day-count blanks are independent. Just don't fill out ¶B if it doesn't apply.

**Q10. If ¶A(2) is checked and closing happens without a release, can Seller sue the lender later to force one?**
A. No — the lender's release is discretionary; Seller can only ASK. TREC 12-3 imposes an obligation to APPLY, not an obligation to succeed. If the lender says no and ¶A(2) is checked, Seller closes and stays on the note. Seller who cares about this outcome should insist on ¶A(1) instead. (TREC 12-3 ¶A(1) / ¶A(2) framing.)
