# TREC 39-11 — Amendment to Contract

**Hadley knowledge file — v1, started 2026-07-01 by hadley_2**
**EFFECTIVE 2026-07-01. Supersedes TREC 39-10 (11-04-2024).**
The post-execution amendment form. Any change to an executed TREC contract (price, dates, repairs, financing terms, etc.) must be papered via 39-11 (or 39-10 for pre-July-1 contracts) — signed by both parties.

---

## PARAGRAPH DELTA — 39-10 vs 39-11

Fields materially unchanged. 39-11 adds clarifying language + one new predefined amendment category.

| 39-10 section | 39-11 section | Change |
|---|---|---|
| §1 Sales Price change | §1 (unchanged) | New sales price $. |
| §2 Down Payment / Cash Portion change | §2 (unchanged) | New cash portion $. |
| §3 Loan Amount change | §3 (unchanged) | New loan amount $. |
| §4 Closing Date change | §4 (unchanged) | New closing date. |
| §5 Possession change | §5 (unchanged) | Possession terms revised. |
| §6 Repairs (¶7.D) modifications | §6 (unchanged) | New repair language. |
| §7 Other Amendments | §7 (unchanged) | Free-text catch-all. |
| §8 (new in 39-11) | §8 **Extension of Option Period** | 39-11 adds a predefined option-period extension category with days-added blank. Practitioners were routinely writing option extensions in §7 free-text; 39-11 formalizes it. |
| §9 (new in 39-11) | §9 **Extension of Financing Approval Deadline** | 39-11 adds a predefined 40-11 ¶2.A/2.B extension category with days-added blank. Same rationale as §8. |

**Practical impact:** existing 39-10 fill map ports 1:1 to 39-11; add two new predefined sections for option-period + financing-deadline extensions.

## Section-by-section rules

### §Header — Reference to underlying contract

Amendment references:
- Property address.
- Effective Date of the underlying contract.
- Names of Buyer + Seller (must match exactly).

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_property_address`, `amendment_underlying_contract_effective_date`, `amendment_buyer_names`, `amendment_seller_names`.

### §1 Sales Price

Change to §3 of underlying contract. New sales price $____. Cash portion + loan portion must reconcile.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_new_sales_price`, `amendment_new_cash_portion`, `amendment_new_loan_amount`.

### §2 Down Payment / Cash Portion

Change to §3.A of underlying contract. New cash portion $____.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_new_cash_portion` (same as §1).

### §3 Loan Amount

Change to §3.B / 40-11 ¶4-8 loan amount. New loan amount $____.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_new_loan_amount` (same as §1).

### §4 Closing Date

Change to §9.A of underlying contract. New Closing Date: ____/____/____.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_new_closing_date`.

### §5 Possession

Change to §10 of underlying contract. New possession terms.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_new_possession_terms` (free-text).

### §6 Repairs (¶7.D modifications)

Change to §7.D of underlying contract. Buyer + Seller agree Seller shall (or shall not) complete the following repairs:
Free-text repair description.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_repairs_description` (free-text).

### §7 Other Amendments

Free-text catch-all for anything not covered above. Practitioners often use this for special provisions or bespoke terms.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_other_description` (free-text).

### §8 Extension of Option Period (NEW in 39-11)

Option Period under §5.B of underlying contract extended by ____ additional days. New Option Period expires on ____/____/____ at 5:00 p.m.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_option_period_extension_days`, `amendment_new_option_period_end_date`.

### §9 Extension of Financing Approval Deadline (NEW in 39-11)

Deadline under 40-11 ¶2.A / 2.B extended by ____ additional days. New deadline: ____/____/____.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_financing_extension_days`, `amendment_new_financing_deadline`.

### §Signatures

Buyer signature + date. Seller signature + date.

**Fixture keys:** `amendment_buyer_signature`, `amendment_buyer_date`, `amendment_seller_signature`, `amendment_seller_date`.

## Cross-form integration

| Trigger | Attach 39-11 |
|---|---|
| Sales price changes post-execution (appraisal, negotiation) | 39-11 |
| Closing date changes (title, financing delay) | 39-11 |
| Repair scope changes (post-inspection) | 39-11 |
| Option period needs extension (Buyer needs more time) | 39-11 §8 (or §7 free-text pre-39-11) |
| Financing deadline needs extension (lender delay) | 39-11 §9 (or §7 free-text pre-39-11) |
| Buyer terminates — do NOT use 39-11 | Use TREC 38-8 (Notice of Buyer's Termination) instead |
| Seller terminates — do NOT use 39-11 | Use TREC 50-0 (Notice of Seller's Termination) instead |

## Deadline math

39-11 has no independent deadlines. It modifies the underlying contract's deadlines by specifying new dates.

**Effective date of amendment:** Date both parties have signed.

**Rule of construction:** Amendment supersedes conflicting terms in the underlying contract. Terms not addressed in amendment remain in force.

## Common Q&A

**Q1. Do I need an amendment for every little change?**
A: For any material change to the executed contract — yes. Verbal amendments to a real estate contract are unenforceable under Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §26.01 (Statute of Frauds). Paper it.

**Q2. Underlying contract was on 20-18. Do I use 39-10 or 39-11 for amendment?**
A: Post-July-1, use 39-11 even if underlying is 20-18. TREC's guidance is to use the current-effective amendment form regardless of the underlying contract's form version. 39-10 remains valid but 39-11 is safer.

**Q3. Amendment changes closing date — do I need to update anything else?**
A: Verify: (1) financing deadline in 40-11 ¶2 — extend via §9 if needed; (2) survey deadline in 6.C — extend via §7 free-text if needed; (3) title objection deadline in 6.D — extend if needed. Amendments are usually the moment to catch cascading deadline effects.

**Q4. Amendment changes sales price. Does the earnest money need to change?**
A: Not automatically. EM is a fixed dollar amount specified in ¶5.A of underlying contract. If parties want EM to move (e.g., proportionally to new price), address in amendment §7. Typically EM stays.

**Q5. What if Seller signs but Buyer refuses?**
A: Amendment is not effective until BOTH parties sign. Seller's unilateral signature = offer to amend. Buyer's refusal = original contract terms remain.

**Q6. Amendment adds repair credit — how do we treat it at closing?**
A: If Seller is giving Buyer a repair credit (Seller pays a dollar amount at closing in lieu of doing repairs), draft in §7 as: "Seller shall credit Buyer $X at closing in lieu of repairs to [item]." Title company + lender will need to allocate on the CD.

**Q7. Amendment extends closing but Buyer's loan approval expires. Now what?**
A: Extend the financing deadline via §9. If the lender's rate lock has expired, that's between Buyer and lender — Buyer may face higher rate, may need to amend loan terms in 40-11 (via another 39-11 pass).

**Q8. Buyer wants to switch financing type (conventional → FHA) mid-contract. Amendment?**
A: Yes. §7 free-text: "Section 1 of the Third Party Financing Addendum is amended to check box C (FHA Insured Financing) in lieu of box A (Conventional Financing). Section 4 loan amount and terms are amended to reflect FHA financing." Both parties sign.

**Q9. Amendment has typos / wrong dates. Void?**
A: Not automatically. Substantial-compliance rule + parol evidence. Best practice: draft a corrected amendment or a "correction amendment" that references and fixes the prior one.

**Q10. Can amendment happen after Closing Date has passed?**
A: In practice, yes, if closing hasn't actually occurred (contract still alive, just past the calendar deadline). Amendment ratifies new closing date + waives the missed date. Legally cleaner to amend BEFORE closing date passes, but real-world deals routinely have retroactive amendments.

## Common practitioner mistakes

1. **Verbal amendments** — Statute of Frauds. Paper it or it's unenforceable.
2. **Missing party signature** — one-side-signed amendment = not effective.
3. **Amendment doesn't reference underlying contract's Effective Date + property address** — may be void for ambiguity.
4. **Cascading deadline oversights** — changing closing without extending financing/survey/objection deadlines.
5. **Using amendment when termination is intended** — 39-11 is for continuation. Termination uses 38-8 (Buyer) or 50-0 (Seller).
