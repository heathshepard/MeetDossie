## Party fields

Same 5-party structure as 20-19 (Buyer, Seller, Buyer's agent, Listing agent, Escrow Agent) plus:

- **Condo Association** — governing entity for the condo project.
- **Management Company** — often distinct from Association.

**Fixture keys:** reuse `seller_names`, `buyer_names`. Condo-specific: `condo_association_name`, `condo_unit_number`, `condo_common_element_percentage`.

## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules (delta from 20-19)

Most paragraphs mirror TREC 20-19. The unique-to-condo paragraphs:

### ¶2 PROPERTY

- **2.A UNIT** — Unit number + condominium project name + county.
- **2.B COMMON ELEMENTS** — Undivided percentage interest in Common Elements (e.g., 1/125th of the total project). Passes with unit.
- **2.C LIMITED COMMON ELEMENTS** — Specific common areas assigned to the unit (e.g., assigned parking space, storage locker).
- **2.D IMPROVEMENTS** — Fixtures within unit boundaries.
- **2.E ACCESSORIES** — Same list as 20-19.
- **2.F EXCLUSIONS** — Seller-retained.

**Fixture keys:** `condo_unit_number`, `condo_project_name`, `condo_common_element_percentage`, `condo_limited_common_elements`.

### ¶3 SALES PRICE

Same as 20-19.

### ¶4 LEASES

Same as 20-19 (residential + fixture leases). Rare for condo units.

### ¶5 EARNEST MONEY + TERMINATION OPTION

Same as 20-19.

### ¶6 TITLE POLICY AND SURVEY

Same as 20-19, except:
- **6.C SURVEY** — condo unit boundaries are established by the recorded condominium declaration + plat. Surveys are less common (the unit's boundaries are declared, not surveyed).
- **6.E TITLE NOTICES** — condo-specific: (1) MANDATORY MEMBERSHIP IN CONDOMINIUM ASSOCIATION (Prop. Code §82.157 — see ¶7 below).

### ¶7 CONDOMINIUM ASSOCIATION — CONDO-SPECIFIC (replaces 20-19 ¶7.B POA)

This paragraph replaces 20-19 ¶6.E.(2) POA notice with condo-specific mechanism.

- **7.A ASSOCIATION** — Name of Condominium Association: ____. Address: ____. Management Company: ____.
- **7.B DELIVERY OF DOCUMENTS**:
  - ☐ (1) Within ____ days after Effective Date, Seller shall deliver Condominium Documents to Buyer at Seller's expense. Documents include: declaration, bylaws, rules, articles of incorporation, most recent balance sheet + budget, statement of assessments, resale certificate under Prop. Code §82.157.
  - ☐ (2) Buyer has received Condominium Documents (dated ____) prior to Effective Date. Buyer ____ requires an updated resale certificate at ____ 's expense.
  - ☐ (3) Buyer waives receipt of Condominium Documents.
  - ☐ (4) Buyer will obtain Condominium Documents at Buyer's expense within ____ days after Effective Date.
- **7.C BUYER'S RIGHT TO TERMINATE** — 6-day termination right after Buyer receives Condominium Documents (Prop. Code §82.157(c)). If Buyer doesn't terminate, Buyer accepts the documents.

**Fixture keys:** `condo_association_name`, `condo_association_address`, `condo_management_company`, `condo_docs_delivery_option` (N-way mutually exclusive 1/2/3/4), `condo_docs_delivery_days`.

### ¶8 PROPERTY CONDITION

Same as 20-19 (SDN, LBP, As Is, repairs).

**But:** Sec. 5.008 SDN requirement may not apply to condo units in the same way (SDN is for single-unit residential; condo has different disclosure regime under Ch. 82). Some agents attach SDN anyway as best practice.

### ¶9-¶13

Same as 20-19.

### ¶14 CASUALTY LOSS

Similar to 20-19 but references Association's master casualty policy.

### ¶15-¶24

Same as 20-19.

### Page 10 BROKER INFORMATION

Same 5-party structure as 20-19.

### Page 11 ESCROW RECEIPTS

Same 4-receipt structure as 20-19.

## Common Q&A

**Q1. What's in the "resale certificate" under Prop. Code §82.157?**
A: Statement of assessments outstanding, statement of pending litigation, statement of insurance, statement of any right of first refusal exercised by association, current declaration + bylaws + rules, budget + balance sheet. Typically 20-100 pages. Seller pays a small fee (~$100-500) to the association for issuance.

**Q2. Buyer has 6 days to terminate after receiving docs — under contract or statute?**
A: Statutory right under Prop. Code §82.157(c). ¶7.C of TREC 30-19 codifies it. Cannot be waived (§82.157(f) — provision voiding termination right is void).

**Q3. What if the condo association won't deliver the resale certificate in time?**
A: Association has 10 days by statute to deliver after request (§82.157(a)). If they don't, Seller may have to sue for compliance. Practical: extend delivery deadline via TREC 39-11.

**Q4. Special assessment coming next month — who pays?**
A: If assessed BEFORE closing = Seller pays. If assessed AFTER closing = Buyer pays. Grey area if assessment vote is pending — disclose in resale certificate; negotiate at closing.

**Q5. Condo association has right of first refusal. Does that block sale?**
A: Association typically has 30-60 days to exercise ROFR (per declaration). If they don't exercise, sale proceeds. If they exercise, they buy at same price. This is a rare provision.

**Q6. Condo has a rental restriction (owner-occupied only). Buyer intends to rent. Now what?**
A: Buyer is bound by condo declarations post-closing. If declaration prohibits rentals, Buyer cannot rent. Attorney review of declarations recommended before Buyer commits.

**Q7. What about pet restrictions?**
A: Condo declarations often restrict pets. Check declarations before Buyer commits. Buyer with pets should either verify permitted or move on.

**Q8. Assessment delinquency by prior owner — who pays?**
A: Seller pays at closing. Resale certificate should disclose exact amount. If Seller refuses to pay outstanding assessments, closing may not happen (title company won't fund).

**Q9. HOA vs Condo Association — same thing?**
A: No. HOA (Property Owners Association, Ch. 209) is for planned communities (single-family homes). Condo Association (Ch. 82) is specifically for condominium units with common elements. Different governing law, different forms.

**Q10. What if the condo project is a "condo-hotel" or short-term rental building?**
A: Special class. Different tax treatment + often different loan requirements. Escalate to attorney; use of 30-19 may still be appropriate but with special provisions.

## Common practitioner mistakes

1. **Using 20-19 for a condo** — wrong form. Use 30-19. Different disclosure regime.
2. **Attaching 36-11 (POA) to a condo contract** — wrong. Condo uses ¶7 mechanism in 30-19.
3. **Not verifying association's health** — condo associations can go bankrupt. Resale certificate reveals financial condition.
4. **Missing 6-day termination window** — Buyer has 6 days to terminate after receiving docs; day 7 is too late.
5. **Special assessment surprises** — always confirm no pending special assessments at closing.

## Personal expert notes (Hadley)

- Condo transactions are underrated in complexity. The association layer + shared ownership + rental restrictions + special assessments create landmines that don't exist in single-family.
- Practical tip: always review the resale certificate + declaration carefully. 60% of "unexpected" condo issues are disclosed but nobody reads the docs.
- Post-July-1 2026, 30-19 supersedes 30-18. Paragraph deltas mirror 20-19 changes (SDN reference updated to 55-1, LBP to 56-0, water rights to 61-0 when applicable).
