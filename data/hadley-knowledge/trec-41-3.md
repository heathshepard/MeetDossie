## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules

### ¶A — Credit Documentation
- Buyer must deliver credit documentation to Seller within a specified number of days after the Effective Date. That day-count is fillable — enter it in the blank at ¶A. (`TREC 41-3 ¶A`)
- The four pre-printed document types Buyer may be required to deliver are: (1) credit report, (2) verification of employment including salary, (3) verification of funds on deposit, (4) current financial statement. A fifth free-text blank lets the parties add any additional item.
- Buyer authorizes any credit reporting agency to furnish copies of Buyer's credit reports to Seller AND to the noteholder(s) — at Buyer's sole expense.
- Rationale: assumption transfers the debt liability to Buyer; Seller (and the noteholder) need enough documentation to assess Buyer's creditworthiness before consenting to the assumption.

### ¶B — Buyer's Credit Approval
- Two triggers, two remedies:
  1. **Buyer fails to deliver on time.** Seller may terminate at any time before all required credit docs are delivered; earnest money goes to **Seller**. (Seller is protected — Buyer's non-delivery is Buyer's fault.)
  2. **Buyer delivers, but credit is unacceptable in Seller's sole discretion.** Seller may terminate by notice to Buyer **within 7 days after the later of (a) expiration of the delivery period or (b) actual delivery**; earnest money goes to **Buyer**.
- If Seller does not exercise the 7-day termination right in ¶B(2), Seller is deemed to have approved Buyer's creditworthiness. (This is a hard deemer — silence = approval.)

### ¶C — Assumption Terms + Loan Balance Variance
- At Closing, Buyer will assume in writing the existing note(s) and all obligations under the deed(s) of trust securing them.
- ¶C(1) and ¶C(2) let the parties describe **up to two** notes (typical: 1st lien + 2nd lien) — payee name, unpaid balance at closing, and total current monthly payment (P&I plus any reserve deposits).
- Buyer's initial payment is the first payment due after closing.
- **Loan-balance variance rule:** If the actual unpaid principal balance at closing differs from the stated amount, the parties choose one of two adjustments — check ONE box: (a) **Cash payable at closing** is adjusted by the variance, or (b) **Sales Price** is adjusted by the variance. Failing to check will create ambiguity; ALWAYS pick one.
- **Excess variance cap:** If the total principal balance of ALL assumed loans varies by MORE than the dollar amount entered in the "greater than $______" blank, either party may terminate and earnest money is refunded to Buyer — UNLESS the OTHER party elects to pay the excess of the variance. (This is a mutual escape valve for big loan-balance surprises.)
- Seller has **7 days after the Effective Date** to deliver to Buyer: copies of the note(s) to be assumed, the deed(s) of trust, and the most recent loan statement(s). (Hard 7-day deadline — start the clock at Effective Date, not any later.)

### ¶D — Loan Assumption Terms (buyer's escape valves)
Buyer may terminate + get earnest money back if the noteholder requires:
1. An assumption fee in excess of the dollar amount entered for C(1) OR for C(2), AND Seller declines to pay the excess. (Seller can save the deal by covering the fee overrun.)
2. An interest-rate increase above the percentage entered for C(1) OR for C(2). (Two separate percentage blanks — one per lien.)
3. Any other modification of the loan documents.

Practical: fill all four blanks (two dollar caps, two rate caps). Leaving them blank effectively allows unlimited fees / rate hikes without triggering Buyer's escape, which is almost never the parties' intent.

### ¶E — Consent by Noteholder
If the noteholder refuses to consent to the assumption, EITHER party may terminate and earnest money is refunded to Buyer. Note: this is bilateral — the failure to obtain consent releases everyone.

### ¶F — Seller's Liens
UNLESS Seller is released from liability on the assumed note(s), the parties will use a **vendor's lien and deed of trust to secure assumption** — these back up Seller if Buyer defaults on the assumed note. The vendor's lien is automatically released once the noteholder delivers an executed release. Cross-reference: if Seller wants to be released from the underlying assumed note liability, attach **TREC 12-3 — Addendum for Release of Liability on Assumed Loan and/or Restoration of Seller's VA Entitlement**.

### ¶G — Tax and Insurance Escrow
If the noteholder maintains an escrow account (ad valorem taxes, casualty insurance, mortgage insurance premiums), Seller transfers the escrow account to Buyer without any deficiency (Seller can't leave a hole) AND Buyer reimburses Seller for the amount in the transferred account. This is a wash for the parties, but it must actually happen at closing to keep the escrow current.

### ¶H — Authorization to Release Information
(1) Lender(s) authorized to share status of the consent-to-assumption process with Seller, Buyer, or their representatives.
(2) Seller and Buyer authorize lender, Title Company, and Escrow Agent to disclose closing disclosures / settlement statements to the parties' respective brokers and sales agents.

### Notices printed on page 2 (not standalone paragraphs but load-bearing)
- **NOTICE TO BUYER.** Warns Buyer to examine the notes and deeds of trust before signing if future adjustments / monthly payments / interest rates concern them. Buyer's due-diligence duty.
- **NOTICE TO SELLER.** Reminds Seller that "liability to pay the notes assumed by Buyer WILL CONTINUE unless Seller obtains a release of liability from the noteholder." Directs Seller to TREC 12-3 for that purpose. This is the #1 misunderstanding among sellers considering assumption — the underlying note stays in Seller's name unless the lender formally releases them.
- **DUE ON SALE NOTICE.** Explains that most modern notes have a due-on-sale clause allowing the noteholder to accelerate the note upon conveyance. If the noteholder refuses to consent, the noteholder may declare the entire note immediately due and payable — this can blow up the deal.

## Deadline math (Buyer-side clock)

| Event | Clock | Trigger | Recipient |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buyer delivers credit documentation | ___ days (¶A blank) | Effective Date | Seller |
| Seller's right to terminate for late credit docs | Any time before ALL credit docs delivered | Late delivery | Buyer (Seller keeps EM) |
| Seller's right to terminate for unacceptable credit | 7 days | Later of (a) expiration of delivery period or (b) actual delivery | Buyer (EM refunded) |
| Seller delivers copies of note(s), DOT(s), most recent statement(s) | 7 days | Effective Date | Buyer |
| Either party terminates for excess variance | Prior to closing | Loan balance variance > cap in ¶C | Earnest money refunded to Buyer (unless other party covers excess) |
| Either party terminates for noteholder non-consent | Prior to closing | Noteholder refuses consent | Earnest money refunded to Buyer |

If any deadline lands on a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday, the standard TREC 20-18 ¶24 rule applies (extend to next day that is not a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday), because 41-3 rides on top of the master contract.

## Common Q&A a working TX agent would ask

**Q1. My buyer client wants to assume the seller's VA loan. Do I only attach 41-3?**
A. No. Attach BOTH 41-3 (Loan Assumption Addendum) and 12-3 (Release of Liability / VA Entitlement Restoration) if the seller wants to be released from future liability and/or restore VA entitlement. Assumption without a release leaves the seller on the hook for the note forever. (TREC 41-3 ¶F NOTICE TO SELLER cross-reference.)

**Q2. What earnest money outcome if the credit documentation is late?**
A. Seller keeps the earnest money if they exercise the ¶B termination right before the documentation is fully delivered. Seller's remedy is terminate + retain EM — Buyer's non-delivery is treated as Buyer's fault. (TREC 41-3 ¶B.)

**Q3. What if the loan balance at closing differs from what we wrote in ¶C(1)?**
A. Two-part answer. First, the difference automatically adjusts either the cash-payable-at-closing or the Sales Price — whichever box is checked. Second, if the difference exceeds the "greater than $____" cap, either party can terminate (EM refunded to Buyer) unless the other party covers the excess. Practical tip: pick a variance cap that reflects a normal principal-paydown pace (a few hundred bucks max between contract and closing) — don't leave it blank. (TREC 41-3 ¶C.)

**Q4. The noteholder says the assumption fee is $2,500 but we capped it at $500 in ¶D(1). What now?**
A. Buyer may terminate and get EM refunded — UNLESS the Seller elects to pay the $2,000 excess. That gives Seller a one-shot chance to save the deal by covering the overrun. (TREC 41-3 ¶D(1).)

**Q5. What's the deemer effect if my Seller doesn't respond within 7 days of receiving the credit docs?**
A. Seller is deemed to have approved the Buyer's creditworthiness. Silence = yes. Seller cannot come back later and try to terminate under ¶B(2). Calendar the 7-day window when the credit docs land. (TREC 41-3 ¶B(2).)

**Q6. Does 41-3 stack with 40-11 (Third Party Financing)?**
A. Not typically — they are alternative financing paths at TREC 20-18 ¶3.B. But if Buyer is BOTH assuming an existing loan AND financing the balance with a new third-party loan, both addenda may attach. Rare, and best practice is to loop in a Texas real estate attorney or the escrow officer before signing this configuration. (Cross-form: TREC 20-18 ¶3.B mutually exclusive checkboxes in normal practice.)

**Q7. Buyer's lender is trying to bump the interest rate from 3.5% to 4.5% — can we terminate?**
A. If the ¶D(2) rate cap was 3.5% in C(1), yes — Buyer may terminate and EM is refunded. The interest-rate cap operates automatically once triggered. Blank ¶D(2) fields = no cap = no escape. (TREC 41-3 ¶D(2).)

**Q8. Do I need the noteholder's affirmative written consent?**
A. Effectively yes — the deal is contingent on the noteholder's consent under ¶E. Practical: get consent in writing before closing to avoid a due-on-sale acceleration risk later. If consent is refused, either party can terminate and EM is refunded to Buyer. (TREC 41-3 ¶E, DUE ON SALE NOTICE.)

**Q9. What happens to the property tax escrow at closing?**
A. Seller transfers the escrow account to Buyer with no deficiency; Buyer reimburses Seller for the balance. Handled at settlement — the escrow officer will proration this on the CD. (TREC 41-3 ¶G.)

**Q10. Is there a form change coming that I should watch for?**
A. As of 2026-07-01, TREC 41-3 (11-07-2022) remains current. Watch `trec.texas.gov/agency-information/legal-resources/advisories-news-and-alerts` and Broker-Lawyer Committee updates for any new promulgation. If a 41-4 issues, this file needs an appendix + supersedence note.
