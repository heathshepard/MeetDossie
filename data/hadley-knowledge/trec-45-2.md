## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules

### ¶A — Definition of "short sale"
"Short sale" means that:
1. Seller's net proceeds at closing will be insufficient to pay the balance of Seller's mortgage loan; AND
2. Seller requires:
   (a) The consent of the lienholder to sell the Property pursuant to this contract; AND
   (b) The lienholder's agreement to:
       (i) accept Seller's net proceeds in full satisfaction of Seller's liability under the mortgage loan; AND
       (ii) provide Seller an executed release of lien against the Property in a recordable format.

Both (a) and (b) are required. Lienholder consent + acceptance of shortfall + recordable release.

### ¶B — Definition of "Seller's net proceeds"
"Seller's net proceeds" means the Sales Price LESS Seller's Expenses under Paragraph 12 of the contract AND Seller's obligation to pay any brokerage fees.

Cross-reference to TREC 20-18 ¶12 (Settlement and Other Expenses). Broker fees come out too. Everything is net-net.

### ¶C — Immediate binding effect + earnest money / option fee obligation
- Contract is binding upon execution by the parties.
- Earnest money AND Option Fee MUST be paid as provided in the contract (i.e., Buyer performs these first-day obligations even though the deal is contingent on lienholder consent).
- Contract is CONTINGENT on satisfaction of ¶A(2) (Lienholder's Consent and Agreement).
- Seller obligations: apply PROMPTLY for and make EVERY REASONABLE EFFORT to obtain Lienholder's Consent and Agreement; furnish all information/documents required by lienholder.
- **Except as provided by this Addendum, neither party is required to perform under the contract while it is contingent upon obtaining Lienholder's Consent and Agreement.**

Translation: during the pending-consent period, deadlines in the main contract don't run except for those specified (like the earnest money/option fee).

### ¶D — Deadline to obtain lienholder consent + Amended Effective Date
- Seller must notify Buyer that Seller has obtained Lienholder's Consent and Agreement on or before ______ (fillable date).
- If NOT obtained by this date, THIS CONTRACT TERMINATES and earnest money is refunded to Buyer.
- Seller must notify Buyer IMMEDIATELY if Lienholder's Consent and Agreement is obtained.
- **Amended Effective Date:** for purposes of performance, the effective date of the contract changes to the date Seller provides Buyer notice of the Lienholder's Consent and Agreement. All time-based deadlines (except option period; see ¶F) restart from the Amended Effective Date.

### ¶E — Lienholder withdrawal
If lienholder REFUSES or WITHDRAWS its Consent and Agreement prior to closing and funding, contract terminates + earnest money refunded to Buyer. Seller must promptly notify Buyer.

### ¶F — Unrestricted right to terminate (Buyer's option period mechanic)
If Buyer has the unrestricted right to terminate (TREC 20-18 ¶5 option period), the time for giving notice of termination:
- BEGINS on the effective date of the contract (original signing);
- CONTINUES after the Amended Effective Date;
- ENDS upon expiration of Buyer's unrestricted right to terminate under Paragraph 5.

**Critical:** Buyer's option period is CUMULATIVE — it doesn't restart with the Amended Effective Date. If Buyer paid for a 10-day option and 6 days passed pre-consent, Buyer has 4 days remaining post-consent. This differs from other TREC amendments where deadlines fully reset.

### ¶G — Time is of the essence
"For the purposes of this Addendum, time is of the essence. Strict compliance with the times for performance stated in this Addendum is required."

Time-is-of-the-essence in short sales is critical because lienholder processing timelines are long and unpredictable. If a party misses a deadline, forgiveness is unlikely.

### ¶H — Authorization to release information
Seller authorizes any lienholder to furnish to Buyer or Buyer's representatives information relating to the status of the request for Lienholder's Consent and Agreement. Buyer can call the lienholder directly for status.

### ¶I — Multiple lienholders
If there is more than one lienholder or loan secured by the Property, THIS ADDENDUM APPLIES TO EACH LIENHOLDER. Each must consent and agree. Failure of ANY lienholder to consent = contract terminates.

## Deadline math

| Event | Clock | Trigger | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contract binding | Signature | Execution | Buyer must deliver EM + option fee per contract |
| Seller applies for lienholder consent | Promptly | Effective Date | Seller's ¶C affirmative obligation |
| Lienholder consent obtained | Seller notifies Buyer immediately | Consent granted | Amended Effective Date = notification date |
| Deadline for lienholder consent | ¶D fillable date | Consent NOT obtained | Contract terminates + EM refunded to Buyer |
| Lienholder withdrawal | Prior to closing/funding | Withdrawal event | Contract terminates + EM refunded to Buyer |
| Buyer's unrestricted right to terminate | Runs continuously | ¶F rule | Not paused by contingency; cumulative from Effective Date |
| Contract deadlines (excluding option) | Post Amended Effective Date | ¶D + ¶C | Restart from Amended Effective Date |

## Common Q&A a working TX agent would ask

**Q1. What makes a sale a "short sale" under TREC 45-2?**
A. When the Seller's net proceeds (Sales Price minus expenses per ¶12 minus brokerage fees) will be INSUFFICIENT to pay off the mortgage balance, AND Seller therefore needs the lienholder to (a) consent to the sale, (b) accept the shortfall in full satisfaction of the loan, and (c) provide a recordable release. All three conditions define a short sale. (TREC 45-2 ¶A + ¶B.)

**Q2. How long does a short sale approval typically take?**
A. Not codified in the form. Practically, 60-120+ days is common; some lienholders push much longer, especially with multiple lienholders. Set ¶D deadline conservatively — 90+ days is often realistic. TREC unverified as a codified benchmark.

**Q3. If the lienholder rejects, does the buyer get their earnest money back?**
A. Yes. Under ¶E, if lienholder refuses OR withdraws consent, contract terminates and EM is refunded to Buyer. Under ¶D, if lienholder doesn't respond by the deadline, same outcome — contract terminates + EM refund.

**Q4. How does the option period work in a short sale?**
A. Cumulative — NOT restarted from the Amended Effective Date. ¶F says the option period BEGINS on the original Effective Date and CONTINUES past the Amended Effective Date. If Buyer paid for a 10-day option and 8 days passed pre-consent, Buyer has 2 days remaining post-consent to exercise the unrestricted right to terminate. Most contract deadlines restart at Amended Effective Date, but not the option period.

**Q5. Buyer wants to terminate DURING the consent-pending period. Can they?**
A. Yes — Buyer's option period runs continuously per ¶F. If Buyer is within the option window, Buyer can exercise the unrestricted right to terminate at any time (pre- OR post-consent). Buyer forfeits only the option fee, not the earnest money.

**Q6. What if the property has TWO mortgages — both need consent?**
A. Yes. ¶I explicitly says the addendum "applies to each lienholder." Both (or all) lienholders must consent + agree + release. Failure of any one = contract terminates. Practical: primary lienholder usually needs to approve the second lien's payoff amount too, so coordination is critical.

**Q7. Seller's expenses under ¶12 — what counts?**
A. Reference is to TREC 20-18 ¶12 (Settlement and Other Expenses). This includes title policy costs (per allocation), escrow fees, tax certificates, recording fees, HOA transfer fees, and other closing costs allocated to Seller. Broker fees come out separately per ¶B. What's LEFT is Seller's net proceeds — the amount going to the lienholder.

**Q8. Does the option fee get refunded if lienholder rejects?**
A. Under ¶D and ¶E, "earnest money will be refunded to Buyer" — but the option fee is separate and non-refundable by TREC 20-18 ¶5 default (it's consideration for Buyer's unrestricted right). TREC 45-2 doesn't override that. Buyer keeps the option value; loses the fee.

**Q9. Time is of the essence — what does that mean practically?**
A. Strict compliance with the deadlines. Late performance is a breach even if only by hours. If Seller misses the ¶D deadline for obtaining consent, the deal terminates automatically — no cure period, no grace, no discretion. This is different from most other TREC forms where deadlines carry some flexibility. (TREC 45-2 ¶G.)

**Q10. Can Buyer contact the lienholder directly?**
A. Yes. ¶H authorizes any lienholder to furnish status information to Buyer or Buyer's representatives. Practical: Buyer's agent should get the lienholder's contact from Seller's agent and call periodically for status — Seller's short sale files sometimes languish in queues.
