# TREC 20-19 — One to Four Family Residential Contract (Resale)

**Hadley knowledge file — v1, started 2026-07-01 by hadley_2**
**EFFECTIVE 2026-07-01. Supersedes TREC 20-18 (11-04-2024).**
This is the master residential resale contract Texas REALTORS use for the sale of an existing 1-4 unit residential property. 20-19 is a paragraph-renumbering + addenda-consolidation refresh of 20-18. Field map is materially the same; paragraph anchors move.

---

## PARAGRAPH DELTA — 20-18 vs 20-19

Agents mid-transaction under 20-18 should continue to use 20-18 references until execution. Agents drafting NEW offers on or after 2026-07-01 must use 20-19.

**Material paragraph changes from 20-18 to 20-19:**

| 20-18 anchor | 20-19 anchor | Change |
|---|---|---|
| ¶2.E RESERVATIONS (mineral reservation trigger) | ¶2.E (unchanged) | No move; language identical. |
| ¶3.B financing checkboxes (3 boxes) | ¶3.B (consolidated to 2 boxes) | Third Party Financing + Loan Assumption/Seller Financing consolidated language. The three financing addenda still exist (40-11, 41-3, 26-8) and are still triggered by checking the appropriate box. |
| ¶4.C Natural Resource Leases | ¶4.C (unchanged, paired Y/N pattern preserved) | No move. |
| ¶6.C SURVEY three-option checkboxes | ¶6.C (unchanged) | No move. |

| ¶7.B Seller's Disclosure Notice checkboxes | ¶7.B (unchanged pattern, but references OP-H become 55-1) | Reference-form updated. |
| ¶10 POSSESSION | ¶10 (unchanged) | No move. |

## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules (short reference — see trec-20-18.md for deep notes; deltas noted here only)

### ¶1 PARTIES — unchanged from 20-18

- Fixture keys: `seller_names`, `buyer_names`.
- Use legal names, not nicknames. Include marital recital where community property applies (Tex. Fam. Code §3.002).

### ¶2 PROPERTY — unchanged from 20-18

- 2.A LAND: legal description (Lot, Block, Addition, City, County, address).
- 2.B IMPROVEMENTS: fixtures list including generators (2025 add).
- 2.C ACCESSORIES: window units, stoves, keys, controls (garage/gates/software).
- 2.D EXCLUSIONS: Seller-retained items (free-text).
- 2.E RESERVATIONS: triggers TREC 44-3 if mineral/water/timber reserved.

### ¶3 SALES PRICE — 20-19 CHANGE

- 3.A cash portion, 3.B financing portion (with checkboxes), 3.C total. A + B must equal C.
- **20-19 change:** ¶3.B checkbox layout consolidated. The three financing addenda (40-11, 41-3, 26-8) still exist and are still triggered by checking the appropriate ¶3.B box. Practical drafting is unchanged — check the appropriate box, attach the appropriate addendum.

### ¶4 LEASES — unchanged from 20-18

- 4.A residential leases, 4.B fixture leases (solar, propane, security), 4.C natural resource leases with paired Y/N HAS/has-NOT delivered checkboxes + days-to-terminate blank.
- Locked convention: paired Y/N checkboxes share key `natural_resource_leases_delivered`; engine inverts. Never split into `_not_delivered`.

### ¶5 EARNEST MONEY + TERMINATION OPTION — unchanged from 20-18

- 5.A delivery to Escrow Agent within 3 days of Effective Date.
- 5.A(1) additional earnest money blank.
- 5.A(2) LEGAL HOLIDAY extension (2025-new, carried into 20-19).
- 5.A(3) application order: Option Fee first, then EM, then additional EM.
- 5.A(4) Escrow Agent authorized to release Option Fee to Seller at any time.
- 5.B TERMINATION OPTION: unrestricted right to terminate by 5:00 p.m. (local time) within ____ days after Effective Date. Option Fee NEVER refunded; EM refunded on timely termination.
- 5.C failure to deliver EM: Seller may terminate or invoke ¶15 remedies.
- 5.D failure to deliver Option Fee: Buyer loses unrestricted termination right.
- 5.E time is of the essence.

### ¶6 TITLE POLICY AND SURVEY — 20-19 CHANGE (Water Rights added to notices)

- 6.A Title policy: Seller/Buyer-expense checkbox + Title Company + 9 exceptions.
- 6.B Commitment: 20-day delivery clock; 15-day auto-extension.
- 6.C Survey: three mutually exclusive checkbox options (existing survey / Buyer may obtain / Seller obtains).
- 6.D Objections: Buyer objects within earlier of Closing Date or ____ days after receipt. Cure Period 15 days.
- 6.E Title Notices: (1)-(12) unchanged from 20-18 PLUS **20-19 new (13): Groundwater/Surface Water Rights Notice** — triggers TREC 61-0 (Seller's Disclosure about Groundwater and Surface Water Rights, effective 2026-07-01) if property has water rights considerations.

### ¶7 PROPERTY CONDITION — 20-19 CHANGE (SDN reference updated)

- 7.A access.
- 7.B Seller's Disclosure Notice: three mutually exclusive checkboxes (received / not received + days / not required). **20-19 reference:** SDN is now TREC 55-1 (was OP-H).
- 7.C Lead paint disclosure trigger (pre-1978 built): attach TREC 56-0 (was OP-L).
- 7.D Acceptance of Property Condition: paired (1) "As Is" / (2) "As Is with repairs" checkboxes + repairs blank.
- 7.E lender-required repairs.
- 7.F completion of repairs.
- 7.G environmental matters.
- 7.H residential service contracts: Seller reimbursement of buyer-purchased home warranty $ blank.

### ¶8 BROKERS AND SALES AGENTS — unchanged

- 8.A Broker/Agent required disclosure (familial/fiduciary/ownership) free-text.
- 8.B Brokers' fees in separate written agreements.

### ¶9 CLOSING — unchanged

- 9.A closing date + year blanks.
- 9.B at-closing duties (1)-(5).

### ¶10 POSSESSION — unchanged

- 10.A paired "upon closing and funding" / "according to a temporary residential lease" checkboxes. Triggers 15-7 (Seller's) or 16-7 (Buyer's) if temp lease.
- 10.B smart devices: Seller must transfer credentials.

### ¶11 SPECIAL PROVISIONS — unchanged

- Three-line free-text blank with UPL warning. Factual statements only; no legal drafting by license holders (TRELA §1101.654).

### ¶12 SETTLEMENT AND OTHER EXPENSES — unchanged from 20-18 (12.A(1)(b) carried)

- 12.A(1) Seller's Expenses list; (b) Seller contribution to Buyer's brokerage fees with paired $/% checkboxes (post-NAR settlement, carried over from 20-18); (c) other Buyer's Expenses cap $.
- 12.A(2) Buyer's Expenses.
- 12.B excess-expense termination right.

### ¶13 PRORATIONS — unchanged

### ¶14 CASUALTY LOSS — unchanged

### ¶15 DEFAULT — unchanged

Buyer defaults: Seller may enforce specific performance OR terminate + receive EM as liquidated damages. Seller defaults: Buyer may enforce specific performance OR terminate + receive EM back + pursue damages.

### ¶16 MEDIATION — unchanged

Optional mediation before litigation.

### ¶17 ATTORNEY'S FEES — unchanged

Prevailing party recovers reasonable attorney's fees.

### ¶18 ESCROW — unchanged

- 18.A escrow agent obligations.
- 18.B expenses of escrow.
- 18.C demand for EM release: 15-day no-objection rule (the practical heart of escrow disputes).
- 18.D damages.
- 18.E notices to Escrow Agent.

### ¶19 REPRESENTATIONS — unchanged

Seller reps: no liens undisclosed, no pending litigation, authority to convey.

### ¶20 FEDERAL REQUIREMENTS (FIRPTA) — unchanged

Seller's non-foreign affidavit; withholding if Seller is foreign person (IRC §1445).

### ¶21 NOTICES — unchanged from 20-18

Paired "To Buyer at" / "To Seller at" notice blocks. Each has: multi-line address, Phone (3-char area code split), TWO E-mail/Fax fields, "With a copy to Buyer's/Seller's agent at" line.

Locked convention: `notice_to_buyer_*` and `notice_to_seller_*` prefixes. Distinct from broker addresses on page 10.

### ¶22 AGREEMENT OF PARTIES — 20-19 CHANGE (addenda checklist updated)

Addenda checkbox list updated to reflect new form numbers:

- Third Party Financing Addendum (TREC 40-11) — unchanged
- Seller Financing Addendum (TREC 26-8) — unchanged
- Loan Assumption Addendum (TREC 41-3) — unchanged
- **Addendum for "Back-Up" Contract (TREC 11-9)** — was 11-7
- **Amendment to Contract (TREC 39-11)** — was 39-10
- **Seller's Disclosure Notice (TREC 55-1)** — was OP-H
- **Lead-Based Paint Addendum (TREC 56-0)** — was OP-L
- POA Addendum (TREC 36-11) — unchanged
- Addendum for Right to Terminate Due to Lender's Appraisal (TREC 49-1) — unchanged
- **Seller's Disclosure about Groundwater and Surface Water Rights (TREC 61-0)** — NEW addendum effective 2026-07-01
- Others: 10-6, 15-7, 16-7, 33-2, 34-4, 38-8, 44-3, 47-0, 48-1, 50-0, 51-1, 52-1, 53-0, 57-0, 59-0, 60-0, "Other (list)" — unchanged

### ¶23 CONSULT AN ATTORNEY — unchanged (page 8)

Paired "Buyer's Attorney is" / "Seller's Attorney is" blocks. Each has multi-line address, Phone (area-code split), Fax (area-code split), E-mail.

Locked convention: `buyer_attorney_*` and `seller_attorney_*` prefixes. NEVER conflate with `listing_agent_*` or `other_broker_*`.

### ¶24 EXECUTION — unchanged (page 9)

"EXECUTED the ____ day of ____, 20____ (Effective Date)."
Broker fills in the Effective Date — the date of final acceptance. This is the trigger for every "X days after the Effective Date" clock in the contract.

Two Buyer signature lines + two Seller signature lines.

### Page 10 BROKER INFORMATION — unchanged

Five distinct party blocks (see party fields section above). Inter-broker compensation Disclosure with paired $/% checkboxes.

### Page 11 ESCROW RECEIPTS — unchanged

Four distinct receipt blocks:
1. Option Fee Receipt
2. Earnest Money Receipt
3. Contract Receipt
4. Additional Earnest Money Receipt

Each independently dated + acknowledged by Escrow Agent. Never collapse.

## Common Q&A (10 pairs agents will actually ask)

**Q1. Which contract do I use starting July 1, 2026 — 20-18 or 20-19?**
A: 20-19 for any offer drafted on or after 2026-07-01. 20-18 remains valid for contracts already executed under it — you don't have to re-paper mid-transaction. But every NEW offer post-July-1 must be on 20-19 (22 TAC §537.28; TREC forms mandatory rule).

**Q2. What's actually different from 20-18?**
A: Minimal for practitioners. Paragraph anchors are the same for ¶1-¶10; ¶6.E adds a new water rights notice (13); ¶22 addenda checklist references updated form numbers (11-9, 39-11, 55-1, 56-0, 61-0). Field map ports 1:1 for DossieSign users. Read the paragraph delta table above.

**Q3. The Seller's Disclosure Notice — which form now?**
A: TREC 55-1, effective 2026-05-28 (per Texas REALTORS® May-June 2026 forms update). OP-H is superseded. If you're delivering an SDN in a listing agreement or as part of a contract offer on or after 2026-05-28, use 55-1. (Tex. Prop. Code §5.008 requires the SDN for resale ≥1 year old.)

**Q4. The Lead-Based Paint form?**
A: TREC 56-0, effective 2026-07-01. Federal 24 CFR §35 requires the Lead-Based Paint Disclosure for any pre-1978 dwelling. 56-0 replaces OP-L.

**Q5. Buyer wants back-up position — do I still use 11-7?**
A: No. TREC 11-9 (Addendum for "Back-Up" Contract), effective 2026-07-01, supersedes 11-7. Same operational function — Buyer's contract sits behind a primary; goes to primary if primary terminates. Deadline math identical to 11-7.

**Q6. Amendment to Contract — 39-10 or 39-11?**
A: 39-11 as of 2026-07-01 (supersedes 39-10). If the underlying contract was executed on 20-18, you can still use 39-10 for the amendment because it's amending the older-form contract — but TREC's guidance is to use the current-effective amendment form regardless. Safer play: use 39-11.

**Q7. Do I need to attach the new water rights disclosure (61-0) on every contract?**
A: Only if the property has groundwater/surface-water considerations (rural properties, properties with wells, properties adjacent to surface water bodies). Urban/suburban lots typically don't trigger 61-0. Check with title company at file open — they'll flag it if it applies.

**Q8. If my Seller's home was built in 1985, do I still need to worry about lead paint?**
A: No. Federal 24 CFR §35 requires the Lead-Based Paint disclosure ONLY for dwellings BUILT BEFORE 1978. A 1985 house is exempt. Do not attach TREC 56-0. But — verify the built date from CAD before checking the exemption on ¶7.C.

**Q9. Buyer wants to close in 45 days. What's the "days after Effective Date" math for the standard timeline?**
A: Roughly: Day 0 = Effective Date. Day 3 = EM + Option Fee delivered. Day 7-10 = Option Period ends. Day 20 = Title Commitment delivered. Day 21 = TPF financing contingency ends. Day 40 = Appraisal in hand; 49-1 termination window opens. Day 45 = Closing. Every one of these clocks starts from Effective Date, NOT from contract signing (see ¶24 — Effective Date is the date of final acceptance filled in by the Broker).

**Q10. What's the practical effect of the ¶3.B checkbox consolidation?**
A: Zero to the drafting agent. You still check ONE box for the type of financing your buyer is using, and attach the matching addendum (40-11 for third-party lender, 41-3 for assumption, 26-8 for seller-carry). The consolidation is cosmetic. Application logic in DossieSign fill engine remains: `addendum_third_party_financing`, `addendum_loan_assumption`, `addendum_seller_financing` — mutually exclusive N-way single-select.

## Common practitioner mistakes (locked from 20-18, carried into 20-19)

1. **Wrong form after July 1** — Drafting offers on 20-18 on/after 2026-07-01. This is a TRELA §1101.652(b)(22) discipline risk. Every offer post-July-1 must be on 20-19.
2. **Old addendum numbers in ¶22** — Checking old form numbers (11-7, 39-10, OP-H, OP-L) on 20-19 ¶22. Update the mental map: new numbers are 11-9, 39-11, 55-1, 56-0.
3. **Not attaching 61-0 for rural properties with water rights** — New addendum, easy to miss.
4. **All the 20-18 mistakes carry** — see trec-20-18.md for the full list (nicknames vs legal names, missing marital recital, MLS-only legal description, unchecked ¶3.B, forgetting solar lease under ¶4.B, missing 5:00 p.m. option deadline, etc.).
