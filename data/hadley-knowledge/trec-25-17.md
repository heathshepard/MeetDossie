## Party fields

Same 5-party structure as 20-19 (Buyer, Seller, Buyer's agent, Listing agent, Escrow Agent) plus:

- **Existing tenants** may be identified in specific paragraphs (grazing lease, farm operator lease).
- **Government agencies** may be referenced (NRCS conservation programs, USDA CRP contracts).

**Fixture keys:** reuse `seller_names`, `buyer_names`. Farm-specific: `farm_grazing_lessee_names`, `farm_conservation_program`, `farm_agricultural_valuation_status`.

## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules (delta from 20-19)

Most paragraphs mirror TREC 20-19. The unique-to-farm-ranch paragraphs:

### ¶2 PROPERTY

- **2.A Land** — same structure as 20-19 (legal description). But farm/ranch legal descriptions are often metes-and-bounds rather than lot-and-block.
- **2.B Improvements** — same list plus barn, corral, fence, etc.
- **2.C Accessories** — same, plus farm-specific.
- **2.D Exclusions** — Seller-retained (e.g., specific farm equipment, harvested crops, livestock).
- **2.E RESERVATIONS** — critical for farm/ranch: **minerals, water rights, timber**. Attach TREC 44-3 if reserving minerals.
- **2.F CROPS + FARM PRODUCTS** — check whether growing crops convey with sale or are retained by Seller.

**Fixture keys:** `farm_growing_crops_convey` (paired Y/N).

### ¶3 SALES PRICE

Same as 20-19 (cash + financing portions).

### ¶4 LEASES (EXPANDED)

Farm/ranch leases include:
- **4.A Residential Leases** — same as 20-19.
- **4.B Fixture Leases** — same as 20-19.
- **4.C Natural Resource Leases** — oil, gas, mineral, geothermal, water, wind, timber.
- **4.D Agricultural Leases** — grazing leases, farm operator leases, hunting leases. **Farm-specific paragraph** not in 20-19.

Seller must deliver copies within ____ days after Effective Date; Buyer may terminate within ____ days after receipt if unacceptable.

**Fixture keys:** `farm_ag_leases_present` (Y/N), `farm_ag_leases_delivery_days`, `farm_ag_leases_termination_days`.

### ¶5 EARNEST MONEY + TERMINATION OPTION

Same as 20-19.

### ¶6 TITLE POLICY AND SURVEY

Same structure as 20-19, PLUS:
- **6.E TITLE NOTICES** — farm-specific: (1) agricultural valuation status; (2) endangered species / wildlife regulations; (3) water rights; (4) grazing leases.
- Farm/ranch surveys typically MUST be new (existing survey often outdated or nonexistent for large acreage). Practitioners often use ¶6.C(3) (Seller pays for new survey).

### ¶7 PROPERTY CONDITION

Same as 20-19 (SDN, LBP, As Is, repairs).

**But:** Sec. 5.008 SDN requirement may not apply if property is >1 dwelling unit and used for agricultural purposes. Check exemption in §5.008(e).

### ¶8-10

Same as 20-19 (brokers' fees, closing, possession).

### ¶11 SPECIAL PROVISIONS

Farm/ranch deals commonly use ¶11 for:
- Ag exemption continuation representations.
- Wildlife management valuation (§23.51 Tax Code).
- Timber contract references.
- Government program contracts (CRP, EQIP).

### ¶12 SETTLEMENT

Same as 20-19.

### ¶13 PRORATIONS

Same as 20-19, plus grazing lease rent proration + crop share proration.

### ¶14 CASUALTY LOSS

Same as 20-19.

### ¶15-¶20

Same as 20-19.

### ¶21 NOTICES

Same as 20-19.

## Common Q&A

**Q1. Property is 100 acres with 1 house — use 20-19 or 25-17?**
A: Depends on primary use. If primarily residential (large residential lot), 20-19 may be appropriate. If primarily agricultural (livestock, farming), 25-17 is correct. Rule of thumb: >10 acres = 25-17. <5 acres with residential = 20-19. 5-10 acres = judgment call based on use.

**Q2. Seller has an ag-exemption on the property. Does that transfer to Buyer?**
A: Not automatically. Buyer must apply to the appraisal district within 30 days of acquisition to continue ag valuation (Tex. Tax Code §23.51). If Buyer changes use to non-agricultural, rollback taxes apply — Buyer pays taxes on non-ag valuation for the prior 5 years.

**Q3. Property has an oil/gas lease from 2019. Does that convey?**
A: Yes, the leasehold interest passes with the sale unless Seller reserves it. Buyer's rights and obligations under the existing lease transfer. Seller should disclose lease terms in ¶4.C.

**Q4. Water rights — who owns them?**
A: Depends on: (a) whether Seller has severed water rights via prior conveyance; (b) surface water rights (permitted by TCEQ) vs groundwater rights (governed by local groundwater conservation district). TREC 61-0 (effective 2026-07-01) is the required disclosure. Complex — often requires water-rights attorney review.

**Q5. Buyer wants to preserve wildlife valuation. What do we do?**
A: Wildlife management valuation (Tex. Tax Code §23.51(7)) requires Buyer to continue qualifying wildlife management activities. Buyer applies within 30 days of acquisition. Seller's wildlife management plan (if any) should be delivered to Buyer for continuity.

**Q6. Existing grazing lease — does Buyer honor it?**
A: Yes. Grazing lease survives sale (like residential lease). Buyer becomes landlord. Terms of grazing lease bind Buyer. Rent proration handled at closing.

**Q7. CRP (Conservation Reserve Program) contract — does that transfer?**
A: CRP contracts are with USDA. Transfer requires USDA approval + Buyer's willingness to continue enrollment. If Buyer opts out, early-termination penalties apply. Practical tip: coordinate with local FSA (Farm Service Agency) office before closing.

**Q8. Property has a mineral lease paying royalties. Do royalties transfer with sale?**
A: Depends on the "surface vs mineral estate" split. If Seller owns both surface AND minerals AND royalties, royalties transfer unless Seller reserves via TREC 44-3. If Seller owns surface only (minerals previously severed), royalties belong to mineral owner not Seller.

**Q9. Endangered species survey — required?**
A: Not always mandatory, but common in rural transactions. If property has habitat for listed species (e.g., golden-cheeked warbler in Central Texas), Buyer may want an environmental assessment. TREC 28-2 governs.

**Q10. Farm + ranch closing — typical timeline?**
A: 45-90 days is typical. Longer than residential due to: title complexity (metes-and-bounds surveys, mineral title, water rights), regulatory (SBA/USDA financing takes longer than conventional), environmental (surveys, testing), tax (ag valuation transfer). Set realistic Closing Date at ¶9.A.

## Common practitioner mistakes

1. **Using 20-19 for a farm/ranch** — wrong form. Use 25-17 for rural/agricultural.
2. **Not addressing minerals** — Seller's mineral ownership is often unclear (previously severed). Verify at title company before drafting ¶2.E.
3. **Not attaching 61-0 for property with water wells** — new effective 2026-07-01.
4. **Ignoring ag exemption / wildlife valuation** — Buyer's failure to apply within 30 days = rollback taxes.
5. **Not verifying grazing lease** — undisclosed grazing lease surfaces post-closing = title dispute.
6. **Metes-and-bounds legal description errors** — MLS descriptions are unreliable; use survey or title commitment.
