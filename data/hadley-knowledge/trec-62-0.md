## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules

### Header — parties
"CONCERNING THE CONTRACT FOR THE SALE OF THE PROPERTY AT ______ BETWEEN THE UNDERSIGNED SELLER AND ______ (BUYER)"

Property address + Buyer name identified.

### Body — Seller's notice (three numbered statements)
"In accordance with Paragraph H of the Addendum for 'Back-Up' Contract, Seller notifies Buyer that:"
1. **The First Contract is terminated.** (Trigger event that activates the back-up.)
2. **The Back-Up Contract with Buyer is no longer subject to the contingency described in Paragraph G of the Addendum for "Back-Up" Contract.** (The back-up is now the primary.)
3. **The Amended Effective Date of the Back-Up Contract is ______** (insert date when the notice is delivered in accordance with Paragraph 21 of the contract).

Reference is to TREC 20-18 ¶21 (Notices) for delivery mechanics. The Amended Effective Date is the DATE OF DELIVERY of this notice — that's when all contract clocks in the Back-Up Contract restart (option period, financing period, closing date offset, etc.).

## Common Q&A a working TX agent would ask

**Q1. When does Seller send 62-0?**
A. Immediately upon termination of the First Contract. Under TREC 38-7 ¶H, Seller has an obligation to notify the back-up Buyer that the primary contract has fallen through. Sending 62-0 activates the back-up. Delay = extended risk of Buyer walking (Buyer has a right to terminate under 38-7 ¶H before Seller gives this notice).

**Q2. What's the Amended Effective Date and why does it matter?**
A. The Amended Effective Date is the DATE 62-0 IS DELIVERED to Buyer. All time-based clocks in the Back-Up Contract (which had been "paused" while in back-up posture) restart from this date. Buyer's new option period, financing period, and closing date offset all count from the Amended Effective Date, not from the original signing date. (TREC 62-0 ¶3; TREC 38-7 ¶H.)

**Q3. Buyer already paid an option fee when signing the Back-Up Contract. Does an "additional" option fee mean a second fee?**
A. Yes. Typically the initial option fee at Back-Up signing covered a very short period. Upon activation via 62-0, Buyer pays ADDITIONAL option fee for the newly-primary contract's option period. The escrow receipt on 62-0 documents this second payment. Amount + terms are negotiated in TREC 38-7 (or via the amendment).

**Q4. Does Buyer have to sign 62-0?**
A. No — the form has SELLER signature lines only. It's Seller's unilateral NOTICE to Buyer. Buyer's counter-signature isn't required. However, the escrow agent DOES sign to acknowledge additional option fee and additional earnest money receipt.

**Q5. What if the First Contract termination is disputed and might reverse?**
A. Don't send 62-0 until termination is confirmed. If Seller sends 62-0 and then the First Contract is reinstated (e.g., mediation, court order, cure), Seller has TWO active primary contracts — a legal disaster. Best practice: verify First Contract termination via signed TREC 38-8 (Notice of Buyer's Termination) or lapsed cure period BEFORE sending 62-0.

**Q6. How is 62-0 delivered?**
A. Per TREC 20-18 ¶21 (Notices) — the notice addresses in ¶21 govern. Typically: email + fax OR certified mail to the address specified. Best practice: multiple methods + preserve delivery receipts. Delivery date = Amended Effective Date.

**Q7. Buyer refuses to accept 62-0 delivery. Can Seller still activate?**
A. Depends on delivery mechanics per ¶21. Refused certified mail is generally treated as delivered upon refusal. Email delivered to the address specified is generally deemed received. Consult attorney if delivery is contested. TREC unverified as codified.

**Q8. Does 62-0 apply if the Back-Up Contract is second in line to a First Contract, and there's actually a Second and Third Contract behind it?**
A. TREC 38-7 handles a bilateral back-up (one back-up behind one primary). Chains of back-ups aren't the standard use case; 62-0 activates ONE back-up. If multiple back-ups exist, each has its own 38-7 + activation notice. Talk to the escrow agent about processing order.

**Q9. What happens to the option period on the Back-Up Contract before 62-0 is delivered?**
A. Buyer's option period is PAUSED while the contract is in back-up posture — the contingency in TREC 38-7 ¶G suspends normal contract mechanics. Upon delivery of 62-0, the option period starts fresh from the Amended Effective Date. Buyer gets full unrestricted right to terminate for the newly-primary option period.

**Q10. Can Seller use 62-0 to notify Buyer of anything OTHER than back-up activation?**
A. No. It's a specific-purpose form for TREC 38-7 back-up contract activation. For other notices, use TREC 39-9 (Amendment to Contract), TREC 38-8 (Termination Notice), or generic written notice per TREC 20-18 ¶21. Don't repurpose 62-0.
