## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules

### NOTICE block (the entire body — a statutory recitation)
The pre-printed NOTICE tells Buyer:
- Property "may be located in a propane gas system service area" authorized to provide propane gas service under Chapter 141, Utilities Code.
- If Property IS in such an area, there may be "special costs or charges" Buyer must pay before receiving propane gas service.
- There may be a "period required to construct lines or other facilities" necessary to provide propane gas service.
- Buyer is ADVISED to (a) determine if the Property is in a propane gas system service area AND (b) contact the distribution system retailer to determine the cost + service-lead-time.

### Buyer acknowledgment block
Buyer acknowledges receipt of the notice at or before execution of a binding contract for purchase OR at closing.

### Statutory attachment requirement
Section 141.010(a), Utilities Code REQUIRES the notice to include a copy of the notice the distribution system retailer is required to record in the real property records. That recorded-notice copy is ATTACHED to 47-0 as a physical attachment.

**Practical instruction printed on the form:** "Seller can obtain a copy of the required recorded notice from the county clerk's office where the property is located or from the distribution system retailer." — If Seller can't produce it, contact the retailer or county clerk BEFORE attaching 47-0.

## Common Q&A a working TX agent would ask

**Q1. How do I know if the property is in a propane gas system service area?**
A. Ask the seller first. Then check with the local propane distribution retailer or the county clerk's office where the property is located. Chapter 141 Utilities Code service areas are relatively narrow — most Texas residential property is NOT in one. If in doubt, ask the utility company that services the neighborhood.

**Q2. What are these "special costs" the notice references?**
A. Not specified in the form. In practice, propane retailer connection fees, line extension costs, tank rental or purchase, and any local special-assessment cost recovery. The form directs Buyer to "contact the distribution system retailer to determine the cost." (TREC 47-0 NOTICE.)

**Q3. Do I have to attach the recorded distributor notice?**
A. Yes, if the Property is in a propane gas system service area. Section 141.010(a) Utilities Code requires the notice attachment; the form's face explicitly says the "recorded notice is attached." Seller obtains the copy from the county clerk or the retailer. If it's not attached, the form is incomplete. (TREC 47-0.)

**Q4. When does the buyer need to acknowledge — before contract or at closing?**
A. Either is acceptable per the form: "at or before execution of a binding contract for the purchase of the above referenced real property or at the closing." Best practice: get the acknowledgment BEFORE contract execution so the disclosure is baked into the offer/acceptance timeline.

**Q5. Does this replace natural-gas or MUD disclosures?**
A. No. This is only for propane gas system service areas under Chapter 141 Utilities Code. Natural-gas is different — usually no special TREC disclosure required. Municipal Utility Districts trigger TREC 59-0 (Notice to Purchaser of Special Taxing or Assessment District). Public Improvement Districts trigger TREC 53-0. These are independent — check each independently.

**Q6. What if Seller doesn't have the recorded notice and won't get it?**
A. Escalate. Chapter 141 Utilities Code recording is a legal requirement of the propane retailer. If Seller can't produce it: (a) contact the retailer directly (Seller can authorize you), or (b) pull from the county clerk's records. Absent the required attachment, the 47-0 is defective and the Buyer may claim the disclosure was not properly delivered.

**Q7. Can we skip 47-0 if the buyer says "I don't care about propane"?**
A. No. The disclosure is statutorily required. Even if Buyer waives their concern, the notice requirement flows from Chapter 141 Utilities Code, not from Buyer's preference. TREC 47-0 is the promulgated vehicle. (TREC 47-0 statutory basis.)

**Q8. Is there an inspection contingency tied to this?**
A. No — 47-0 is disclosure-only. It doesn't give Buyer a right to terminate. Buyer's protection is knowledge: Buyer signs having been told propane service costs may be significant. If Buyer later objects, remedies come from the main contract (¶7 property condition, option-period termination), not from 47-0.

**Q9. Does the seller have any liability if the disclosure turns out to be wrong?**
A. The form is a recitation of a general statutory advisory, not a Seller representation about specific costs. Seller isn't warranting the cost figure or the timeline — those come from the retailer. Seller IS warranting delivery of the required recorded notice (which is a retailer-generated document). TREC unverified as a general rule; the form's face doesn't create Seller liability for cost accuracy.

**Q10. Is 47-0 due for a promulgation update?**
A. As of 2026-07-01, TREC 47-0 (2-10-2014) is the current version. It's stable and relatively narrow in application. Watch TREC advisories for any refresh, especially if Chapter 141 Utilities Code is amended.
