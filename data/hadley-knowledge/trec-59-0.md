## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules

### Preliminary NOTICE
- "Not for use for Public Improvement Districts (PIDs)." — For PIDs, use TREC 53-0.
- Section 49.453 Tex. Water Code requires each district to make the notice available on its website or otherwise.
- **PRIORITY:** If the district publishes its own form of notice, Seller should use the district's form INSTEAD OF TREC 59-0. Use 59-0 only when the district hasn't published one.

### Header block
Property address (Street Address and City).

### ¶1 — Name of Special District
Insert district name. Property may be subject to district taxes or assessments.

### ¶2 — Tax Rate (Check ONE box)
Two mutually-exclusive checkboxes:
- **Current rate** — insert current property tax rate per $100 of assessed valuation.
- **Projected rate** (district has NOT yet imposed taxes) — insert projected property tax rate per $100.

Note printed on the form: "The district may, subject to voter approval, impose taxes and issue bonds. The district may impose an unlimited rate of tax in payment of such bonds." — VOTER-APPROVED bonds can drive unlimited tax rates for repayment.

### ¶3 — Assessments (Check ONE box)
Three mutually-exclusive options:
- **Current assessment rate** — per $100 of assessed valuation.
- **Current assessment amount** on THIS property.
- **Projected rate OR projected amount** (district has NOT yet imposed) — insert projected figure.

### ¶4 — Bonds (subsections A and B)

**A. Total amounts of bonds APPROVED by voters (payable wholly or partly from property taxes / assessments):**
- Paired checkbox: (i) EXCLUDING refunding bonds separately approved by voters; (ii) EXCLUDING bonds payable solely from revenues under a contract with a governmental entity.
- Four sub-amount fields:
  1. Water, sewer, and drainage facilities: $______
  2. Road facilities: $______
  3. Parks and recreational facilities: $______
  4. Other (insert description): $______

**B. Aggregate initial principal amounts of all such bonds ISSUED (as opposed to just authorized):**
- Same four sub-amount fields.

The AB distinction matters: A shows the voter-authorized ceiling; B shows how much has actually been sold. Delta = future bond capacity.

### ¶5 — Standby Fees
Print the amount of the standby fee (only if the district obtained TCEQ approval to adopt and impose one). Note: "An unpaid standby fee is a personal obligation of the person that owned the property at the time of imposition and is secured by a lien on the property." — Standby fees carry lien risk.

### ¶6 — Location (Check ONE box, if applicable)
Mutually exclusive:
- District is **wholly or partly in the extraterritorial jurisdiction (ETJ)** of City of ______. — Municipality may annex under Texas law; annexed districts may dissolve.
- District is **wholly or partly within the corporate boundaries** of City of ______. — Municipality and district overlap; may not provide duplicate services; property is subject to taxation BY BOTH.

### ¶7 — Strategic Partnership Agreement (Check box, if applicable)
District has entered into a strategic partnership agreement with City of ______. This addresses timeframe/process/procedures for annexation of the ETJ portion.

### ¶8 — Purpose (Check applicable boxes)
District purposes (multi-select):
- Water
- Sewer
- Drainage
- Flood control
- Firefighting
- Road
- Parks and recreational
- Other (insert)

"The cost of district facilities is not included in the purchase price of your property." — District financing = additional obligation on Buyer.

### Closing note printed
"PURCHASER IS ADVISED THAT THE INFORMATION SHOWN ON THIS FORM IS SUBJECT TO CHANGE BY THE DISTRICT AT ANY TIME. THE DISTRICT ANNUALLY ESTABLISHES TAX RATES. PURCHASER IS ADVISED TO CONTACT THE DISTRICT TO DETERMINE THE STATUS OF ANY CURRENT OR PROPOSED CHANGES TO THE INFORMATION SHOWN ON THE FORM."

### Buyer acknowledgment
"The undersigned purchaser hereby acknowledges receipt of the foregoing notice at or before the execution of a binding contract for the purchase of the real property or at closing of purchase of the real property."

## Common Q&A a working TX agent would ask

**Q1. Is a MUD the same as a PID?**
A. No. Different statutes, different disclosure forms:
- **MUD (or WCID, or other Chapter 49 special district)** — Texas Water Code Chapter 49; levies ad valorem TAXES; TREC 59-0.
- **PID** — Texas Local Government Code Chapter 372/382; levies ASSESSMENTS; TREC 53-0.
Both can exist on the same property; attach each applicable form independently.

**Q2. The district has its own notice form. Do I still use 59-0?**
A. No — use the district's form. The 59-0 preamble explicitly says: "If available, Seller should use the district's form instead of this form. If the district does not have the form of notice on its website or does not publish a form of notice, Seller should obtain the information from the district and complete this form with the information from the district."

**Q3. How do I find a district's current tax rate?**
A. Contact the district directly. Most Texas MUDs post current tax rates on their websites. Also try the appraisal district (CAD) records for the property — the MUD line-item tax rate appears on the tax bill.

**Q4. What's the difference between ¶4A and ¶4B bond amounts?**
A. ¶4A = the voter-approved CEILING for each category (total future bond capacity). ¶4B = the aggregate principal amount actually issued to date. Delta = future bond room. Buyer should look at the delta — a MUD with $50M authorized but only $10M issued may drive up tax rates as it issues more bonds. (TREC 59-0 ¶4A / ¶4B.)

**Q5. What are standby fees?**
A. Fees the district can charge — typically to maintain readiness to provide service — even when the property isn't yet receiving service. Requires TCEQ approval. Standby fees are a PERSONAL OBLIGATION of the owner at time of imposition and are secured by a LIEN. Buyer should ask the district if there's an unpaid standby fee before closing; the title company should also flag any lien. (TREC 59-0 ¶5.)

**Q6. The MUD is in a city's ETJ — will the city annex it?**
A. Maybe. Texas law governs annexation, and rules have changed multiple times (most recently under HB 347 / 2019 changes limiting non-consent annexation). If annexed, the district may dissolve or continue depending on the strategic partnership agreement. Recommend checking ¶6 and ¶7 carefully. (TREC 59-0 ¶6, ¶7.)

**Q7. My district has a strategic partnership agreement with the city — what does that mean?**
A. It's a Chapter 49/§43 Local Government Code framework where the city and district agree on how annexation will work, what services will be provided, and how tax revenues will be shared. Buyer inherits the agreement's effects. Sometimes districts continue operating alongside city services; sometimes they wind down over a defined timeline. (TREC 59-0 ¶7.)

**Q8. Do I need 59-0 for a district that levies only assessments (not taxes)?**
A. If the district was created under Chapter 49 Water Code (MUD/WCID/etc.), yes — even if it's currently only assessing rather than taxing. If the district was created under Chapter 372/382 Local Government Code (PID), no — use 53-0 instead. The trigger is enabling statute, not levy mechanism.

**Q9. What if the district hasn't imposed taxes yet?**
A. Use the "projected rate" checkbox in ¶2. Buyer needs the same disclosure — the district CAN impose taxes even if it hasn't yet. Voter-approved bonds may drive unlimited tax rates for repayment; projected rate is Buyer's estimate of future exposure. (TREC 59-0 ¶2.)

**Q10. Can Buyer terminate under 59-0?**
A. No affirmative termination right under 59-0. Buyer's protection is knowledge before signing plus TREC 20-18 ¶5 option-period termination. Best practice: attach 59-0 (or the district's own form) to the initial offer so Buyer prices in the district burden.
