## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules

### ¶A The Property Owners Association

Name of the Property Owners' Association: ____.

**Fixture keys:** `poa_name`.

### ¶B Subdivision Information

**Check ONE:**
- ☐ (1) Within ____ days after Effective Date, Seller shall obtain, pay for, and deliver Subdivision Information (SI) to Buyer.
- ☐ (2) Buyer has received Subdivision Information (dated ____) prior to Effective Date. Buyer ____ requires an updated resale certificate at ____ 's expense.
- ☐ (3) Buyer waives receipt of Subdivision Information.
- ☐ (4) Buyer will obtain SI at Buyer's expense within ____ days after Effective Date.

**Subdivision Information** = the "Subdivision Information Resale Certificate" under Tex. Prop. Code §207.003. Includes: current assessments, delinquent assessments on the Property, any lien for delinquent assessments, restrictions/covenants, financial statements of the association, and any other information the association is required to provide.

**Fixture keys:** `poa_si_delivery_option` (N-way mutually exclusive: 1/2/3/4), `poa_si_days` (blank for options 1 and 4), `poa_si_date_received` (for option 2), `poa_si_update_required` (paired Y/N for option 2), `poa_si_update_expense_party` (Buyer or Seller for option 2).

### ¶C Buyer's Right to Terminate

If Buyer receives Subdivision Information after Effective Date, Buyer may terminate this contract by giving Seller notice within ____ days after Buyer receives the SI, and EM shall be refunded to Buyer. If Buyer does not timely terminate, Buyer accepts the SI.

**Fixture keys:** `poa_termination_days`.

**Typical:** 3-5 days after SI receipt.

### ¶D Fees

**Deposits + fees payable at closing:**
- Regular assessment (annual/monthly dues) — paid by Buyer at closing (prorated per §13 of master contract).
- Special assessments outstanding — paid by ____ (Buyer or Seller).
- Transfer fees / capital contributions — paid by ____ (Buyer or Seller).

**Fixture keys:** `poa_special_assessment_party`, `poa_transfer_fee_party`.

### ¶E Assessments

Notice: assessments are the Buyer's responsibility upon acquisition. Failure to pay may result in POA foreclosure under Tex. Prop. Code §§ 209.0091-0092.

**No fillable fields.** Fixed statutory notice.

### ¶F Amendment or Termination of POA Documents

Seller represents no known pending amendments to POA governing documents. Seller has delivered copies of current declarations/bylaws to Buyer as part of SI.

**No fillable fields.**

## Common Q&A

**Q1. Who pays for the Subdivision Information (SI)?**
A: Depends on ¶B checkbox. Option 1: Seller pays. Option 4: Buyer pays. Option 2: whoever pays for the update (if required) — checkbox specifies. Option 3: N/A (Buyer waived).

**Q2. What's in a "Subdivision Information Resale Certificate"?**
A: Per Tex. Prop. Code §207.003: current assessments on Property, delinquent assessments, liens for delinquent assessments, restrictive covenants, financial statements of POA, insurance information, any pending litigation, and any other required disclosures. Typically 20-100 pages.

**Q3. POA won't deliver SI in time. Can Buyer terminate?**
A: If ¶B option 1 (Seller obtains SI) is checked and Seller doesn't deliver within blank days: Buyer's ¶C termination clock hasn't started (needs receipt), but this may be Seller's breach — enforceable via default provisions. Practical: extend delivery deadline via TREC 39-11 or have Buyer walk under 20-19 ¶6.D (title objections extended for POA delay).

**Q4. Buyer wants to review SI carefully but 3 days isn't enough. Extend?**
A: Extend ¶C days via TREC 39-11 Amendment. Or set a longer initial deadline at drafting — 7-10 days is not unusual for complex POA documents.

**Q5. Buyer waives SI (¶B option 3). Then discovers a $50K special assessment post-closing. Recourse?**
A: Waiver is Buyer's decision at contract signing. Post-closing recourse is limited: (1) fraud claim if Seller knew and hid; (2) POA disclosure claim if POA violated §5.012 duty to disclose. Waivers are dangerous — practitioners should discourage.

**Q6. Special assessment outstanding — who pays?**
A: Whoever is checked in ¶D. Typically Seller pays outstanding special assessments through closing; Buyer picks up any post-closing assessments. Negotiable.

**Q7. Transfer fees — Seller or Buyer?**
A: Typically Buyer pays transfer fees (they're a cost of acquisition). But some POAs assess Seller. Check the POA's fee schedule + negotiate in ¶D.

**Q8. Property has TWO POAs (master association + sub-association). One 36-11 or two?**
A: One 36-11 with both POAs named in ¶A ("Master POA Name AND Sub-POA Name"). SI must include documents from both. If POAs are logically separate, better practice: attach 36-11 twice (once per POA) as separate addenda.

**Q9. Buyer's lender wants the POA's insurance certificate — is that in the SI?**
A: Usually yes. Tex. Prop. Code §207.003 requires insurance info in the resale certificate. If not delivered, Buyer must request separately from POA.

**Q10. HOA (voluntary) vs POA (mandatory). Do I attach 36-11 to a voluntary HOA?**
A: No. 36-11 applies only to MANDATORY membership. Voluntary HOA = master contract ¶6.E.(2) "is not subject to mandatory membership" checkbox → no 36-11. But: verify carefully — most Texas HOAs are actually mandatory (created under Prop Code Ch. 202 restrictive covenants).

## Common practitioner mistakes

1. **Not attaching 36-11 when property is in a POA** — leaves Buyer without SI disclosure right. Failure to disclose can void the contract.
2. **Leaving ¶B days blank** — no deadline = ambiguous SI delivery obligation.
3. **Leaving ¶C days blank** — Buyer's termination right has no time limit → ambiguous, typically deemed waived after closing.
4. **Confusing POA (Ch. 209) with Condo Association (Ch. 82)** — different forms. Condo uses 30-19; POA uses 36-11.
5. **Not verifying assessment status pre-closing** — special assessments can be $10K-$50K+ surprises.

## Personal expert notes (Hadley)

- 36-11 is Buyer's biggest protection against POA surprises. The SI reveals financial health of the POA + any pending assessments — Buyer should ALWAYS review carefully.
- Practical tip: Seller's option (¶B option 1) is almost always used because Seller has faster access to POA management (they're the current owner). Buyer's option (¶B option 4) is rare.
- Waiver (¶B option 3) is nearly never appropriate. Practitioners should discourage — even if Buyer thinks they know the POA, financial surprises are common.
