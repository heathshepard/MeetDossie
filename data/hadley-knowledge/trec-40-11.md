## Paragraph-by-paragraph rules

### ¶1 TYPE OF FINANCING AND DUTY TO APPLY AND OBTAIN APPROVAL

Check ONE of:
- ☐ A. Conventional Financing
- ☐ B. Texas Veterans Land Board Financing (Veterans Housing Assistance Program — VHAP)
- ☐ C. FHA Insured Financing
- ☐ D. VA Guaranteed Financing
- ☐ E. USDA Guaranteed Financing
- ☐ F. Reverse Mortgage Financing
- ☐ G. Other (describe)

Buyer shall apply within ____ days after Effective Date and make every reasonable effort to obtain Buyer Approval. Buyer's failure to apply timely = failure to obtain Buyer Approval.

**Fixture keys:** `tpf_financing_type` (mutually exclusive N-way — each checkbox distinct key), `tpf_application_days`.

**Locked convention:** N-way mutually exclusive selection — separate keys per checkbox (`tpf_conventional`, `tpf_vlb`, `tpf_fha`, `tpf_va`, `tpf_usda`, `tpf_reverse`, `tpf_other`). Application logic enforces single-select.

### ¶2 APPROVAL AND TERMINATION FOR LENDER'S APPROVAL OF BUYER

- **2.A Buyer Approval** — Buyer obtains lender approval based on Buyer's income, creditworthiness, assets, debts. Deadline: ____ days after Effective Date (typically 21-30).
- **2.B Buyer's Right to Terminate** — If Buyer cannot timely obtain Buyer Approval, Buyer may terminate by giving Seller notice AND a copy of Lender's denial letter within the same ____ days after Effective Date. EM refunded on timely termination.
- **Property Approval** — Lender approval of the property (appraisal, condition, marketability). If Property Approval is not obtained, Buyer may terminate under 2.B same clock.

**Fixture keys:** `tpf_buyer_approval_days`, `tpf_termination_notice_deadline_days`.

**Critical rule:** Buyer's termination under 2.B requires BOTH written notice AND lender denial letter within the deadline. Notice alone = insufficient. Denial letter alone = insufficient.

### ¶3 SECURITY

Buyer's loan will be secured by the Property.

**No fillable fields.**

### ¶4 CONVENTIONAL FINANCING (if 1.A checked)

Loan Amount $____, portion of Sales Price payable in cash. Interest rate not exceeding ____% per annum for first ____ years of loan. Amortization period not less than ____ years.

**Fixture keys:** `tpf_conv_loan_amount`, `tpf_conv_max_rate`, `tpf_conv_rate_lock_years`, `tpf_conv_amortization_years`.

### ¶5 TEXAS VETERANS LAND BOARD FINANCING (if 1.B checked)

VLB Loan Amount not less than $____.

**Fixture keys:** `tpf_vlb_min_loan_amount`.

### ¶6 FHA/VA/USDA FINANCING (if 1.C, 1.D, or 1.E checked)

Loan amount, interest rate cap, amortization period fields similar to ¶4. Plus:

**FHA notice** — Buyer + Seller may not be obligated to close if the FHA "as-is" appraised value is less than sales price. Buyer has right to terminate.

**VA notice** — Buyer + Seller may not be obligated to close if the VA reasonable value of the Property is less than sales price. Buyer has right to terminate.

**USDA notice** — similar appraisal / eligibility conditions.

**Fixture keys:** `tpf_fha_loan_amount`, `tpf_va_loan_amount`, `tpf_usda_loan_amount`, plus rate/amortization fields as with ¶4.

### ¶7 REVERSE MORTGAGE FINANCING (if 1.F checked)

Buyer will obtain reverse mortgage; loan proceeds pay Buyer, not Seller. Special HUD counseling requirements. **Rare in Texas resale.**

### ¶8 OTHER

Describe other financing arrangement. Rare.

**Practical timeline:** Buyer applies within 3-5 days. Full approval within 21-30 days. Closing at 30-45 days. Appraisal (if 49-1 attached) is a separate clock — see 49-1 knowledge file.

## Common Q&A

**Q1. Buyer's application clock — when does it start?**
A: Effective Date. Not the date the contract was drafted, not the date Buyer got the offer accepted verbally — the Effective Date filled in on ¶24 of the master contract by the Broker (the date of final acceptance).

**Q2. Buyer got denied at day 22 when the 2.B deadline was day 21. Can they still terminate?**
A: No. Time is of the essence under ¶5.E of the master contract. Missing 2.B by even a day = Buyer forfeits termination right. Buyer must close or default under ¶15 (Seller keeps EM).

**Q3. Buyer got approval but at a rate higher than the ¶4 cap. Can Buyer terminate?**
A: Yes. If Buyer's Approval is not obtained on terms specified in ¶1/¶4-8, Buyer is entitled to treat it as no Buyer Approval and terminate under 2.B — provided Buyer delivers the lender document evidencing the higher-rate approval + timely notice.

**Q4. Property Approval — what does that mean exactly?**
A: Lender's approval of the property itself, separate from Buyer's approval. Appraisal, condition, title, marketability. If the property doesn't meet lender's underwriting standards (e.g., failed appraisal, condition issues that lender won't finance), Buyer may terminate under 2.B.

**Q5. FHA/VA appraisal comes in low — does Buyer have to close?**
A: Not necessarily. FHA/VA loans have "escape clauses" that entitle Buyer to terminate if the appraised value is less than sales price. 40-11 ¶6 preserves this right. Best practice: also attach 49-1 for conventional loans + belt-and-suspenders termination right.

**Q6. Buyer wants to switch from conventional to FHA mid-contract. Amendment?**
A: Yes. TREC 39-11 Amendment. Draft an amendment updating 40-11 ¶1 checkbox from A (Conventional) to C (FHA), signed by both parties.

**Q7. Interest rate cap — what number should Buyer put?**
A: A realistic ceiling that lender can approve at. If Buyer expects 6.5%, cap at 7% to give buffer. Cap too tight = artificial "approval failure" enabling opportunistic termination (which may be construed as bad faith). Cap too loose = Buyer stuck at a rate they didn't budget for.

**Q8. Buyer's loan amount changes after appraisal — how does that flow through?**
A: If sales price stays the same but Buyer's loan drops (appraisal gap), Buyer may need to bring more cash to close. Update ¶4 loan amount via TREC 39-11 Amendment. If Buyer can't cover the gap, 49-1 (if attached) provides termination right.

**Q9. Lender's denial letter — what qualifies?**
A: A formal written denial from lender's underwriting, stating Buyer does not meet approval criteria. NOT a broker email, NOT a text from the loan officer. Denial letter must be lender-generated + tied to underwriting.

**Q10. Buyer never applied for the loan. Can they still terminate under 2.B?**
A: No. ¶1 states Buyer's failure to apply = failure to obtain Buyer Approval. Buyer forfeits termination right AND may be in default under ¶15. Seller may keep EM.

## Common practitioner mistakes

1. **Leaving ¶2.A / ¶2.B days blanks empty** — no deadline = no termination right. Set 21-30 days conventionally.
2. **Buyer missing 2.B deadline by 1 day** — strict compliance. Set calendar reminders.
3. **Rate cap too tight** — bad faith risk if Buyer terminates on a rate that's within market norms.
4. **Not attaching 49-1** — appraisal gap = Buyer's biggest post-financing risk. 49-1 = the safety net.
5. **Cash-portion mismatch** — 3.A cash portion + 40-11 loan amount must equal 3.C sales price.

## Personal expert notes (Hadley)

- 40-11 is the highest-frequency addendum in TX residential resale — 70-80% of buyers use third-party financing. Every agent handles this weekly.
- The rate cap + amortization fields are subtly load-bearing. Practitioners default to "current market + 0.5%" for the rate cap; setting it too tight has caused bad-faith termination disputes.
- Coordination with 49-1 (lender appraisal) is where deals live or die. Best practice is to attach both routinely.
